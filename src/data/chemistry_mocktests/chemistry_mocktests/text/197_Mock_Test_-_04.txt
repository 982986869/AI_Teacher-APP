Q4. (id 21971) [MCQ]
Among the following, the maximum covalent character is shown by the compound is:
   (A) MgCl_(2)
   (B) AlCl_(3)  <-- correct
   (C) SnCl_(2)
   (D) FeCl_(2)

Answer: AlCl_(3)
Explanation:
Few ionic bonds have partial covalent characteristics which were first discussed by a scientist, Kazimierz Fajans in 1923. He gave the rule to predict whether a chemical bond will be covalent or ionic which is now commonly known as Fajan’s rule. The rule can be stated as:

 Size of the ion: Smaller the size of cation, larger the size of anion, greater is the covalent character of the ionic bond.
 Charge of Cation: Greater the charge of cation, greater is the covalent character of the ionic bond.
 Electronic configuration- For cations with the same charge and size, the one, with (n-1)d^(n)ns^(​​​0) which is found in transition elements, has greater covalent character than the cation with ns^(2)np^(6) electronic configuration, which is commonly found in alkali or alkaline earth metals.

Keeping these rules in mind, we can say that AlCl_(​​​3) has a high covalent character.

   (A) equal to or greater than h/ $\pi$
   (B) equal to or greater than h/4 $\pi$ .  <-- correct
   (C) equal to or greater than h/3 $\pi$
   (D) equal to or greater than h/2 $\pi$

Answer: equal to or greater than h/4 $\pi$ .
Explanation:
The uncertainty principle says that we cannot measure the position (x) and the momentum (p) of a particle with absolute precision. Multiplying together the errors in the measurements of these values has to give a number greater than or equal to half of a constant called "h-bar". This is equal to Planck's constant (usually written as h) divided by 2 $\pi$ .

 $Δx. Δp \ge { h\over 4\pi}$

The commonly occurring isotopes of carbon atoms are: ${_{\text{6}}^{{\text{12}}}}{\text{C }}{,_{\text{6}}}^{{\text{13}}}{\text{C }}{,_{\text{6}}}^{{\text{14}}}{\text{C}}$ . They contain:
   (A) 7, 8, and 9 neutrons 6 protons

   (C) 6, 7, and 9 neutrons and 6 protons
   (D) 6, 8, and 9 neutrons and 6 protons

Answer: 6, 7, and 8 neutrons and 6 protons
Explanation:
Isotopes are atoms with the same number of protons but that have a different number of neutrons.

The atomic number of carbon = 6.

So, the number of protons in each isotope = 6.

Number of neutrons in $^{12}_6C$ = 12-6= 6.

Number of neutrons in $^{13}_6C$ = 13-6= 7.

Number of neutrons in $^{14}_6C$ = 14-6= 8.

Q52. (id 69556) [Numerical/MCQ]
_______ involves separation of a mixture over a column of adsorbent packed in a glass tube.
   (A) Partition chromatography
   (B) Thin layer chromatography
   (C) Gas chromatography
   (D) Column chromatography  <-- correct

Answer: Column chromatography
Explanation:
Column chromatography involves the separation of a mixture over a column of adsorbent (stationary phase) packed in a glass tube. The column is fitted with a stopcock at its lower end. The mixture adsorbed on soluble adsorbent is placed on the top of the adsorbent column packed in a glass tube. An appropriate eluant which is a liquid or a mixture of liquids is allowed to flow down the column slowly. Depending upon the degree to which the compounds are adsorbed, complete separation takes place.

Q53. (id 594755) [MCQ]
Instructions for Question 53 to 55:
A qualitative measure of the ability of an atom in a chemical compound to attract shared electrons to itself is called electronegativity. Unlike ionization enthalpy and electron gain enthalpy, it is not a measurable quantity. However, a number of numerical al scales of electronegativity of elements viz., Pauling scale, Mulliken-Jaffe scale, Allred-Rochow scale have been developed. In 1922 assigned arbitrarily a value of 4.0 to fluorine, the elements considered to have the greatest ability to attract electrons. The electronegativity of any given element is not constant; it varies depending on the element to which it is bound. Though it is not a measurable quantity, it does provide a means, strong tendency to gain electrons.

 ![image](https://media-mycbseguide.s3.amazonaws.com/images/imgur/1632228328-cdmarv.jpg) 

Therefore, electronegativity is directly related to the non-metallic properties of elements. It can be further extended to say that electronegativity is inversely related to the metallic properties of elements. Thus, the increase in electronegativities across a period is accompanied by an increase in non-metallic properties (or decrease in metallic properties) of elements

Q54. (id 594756) [MCQ]
In which of the following options the order of arrangement does not agree with the variation of the property indicated against it?
   (A) B < C < N < O (increasing first ionization enthalpy)
   (B) Al^(3)+ < Mg^(2)+ < Na^(+) < F^(-) (increase ionic size)
   (C) I< Br < CI < F (increasing electron gain enthalpy)  <-- correct
   (D) Li < Na < K < Rb (increasing metallic radius)

Answer: I< Br < CI < F (increasing electron gain enthalpy)
Explanation:
Electron gain enthalpy decreases from top to bottom in a group but due to small size of fluorine it has lower electron gain enthalpy than chlorine. Hence, the correct order is I< Br < F < Cl.

Also, ionization enthalpy increases on moving towards right along a period but due to extra stability of nitrogen due to half-filled outer orbital there is higher exchange energy hence, it achieves higher stability due to which it has higher ionization energy than oxygen. Therefore, the correct order is B < C < O < N.
