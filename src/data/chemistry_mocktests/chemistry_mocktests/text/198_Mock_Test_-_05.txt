Thus N_(2)

 The triple bonded N_(2) with bond order = 3 has the highest bond dissociation energy.
 The doubly bonded O_(2) with bond order = 2 has lesser bond dissociation energy than N_(2)
 F_(2) and Cl_(2) both are single bonded having the same bond order = 1 

But, the electron-electron repulsive force (e-e repulsion) is maximum in the F_(2) molecule due to its small size. This accounts for the lower bond dissociation energy of F_(2 ) as compared to Cl_(2).
