Q15. (id 61921) [Numerical/MCQ]
According to Gay Lussac’s Law of Gaseous Volumes, " when gases combine or are produced in a chemical reaction they do so in a ______ ratio by volumes provided all gases are at the same temperature and pressure."
   (A) simple  <-- correct
   (B) equal
   (C) inverse
   (D) uncertain

Answer: simple
Explanation:
Gay lussac’s law states that when gases combine or are produced in a chemical reaction they do so in a simple ratio by volume provided all gases are at the same temperature and pressure.

Mathematically, the law is expressed as,

[V/n] = k

where V represents the volume of the gas, n denotes the number of moles of the gas & k is the proportionality constant.

Thus, the volume ratio must be related to the relative number of molecules.

This relationship was crucial in establishing the formula of simple molecules at a time when a distinction between atoms and molecules was not clearly understood.

Q38. (id 21966) [MCQ]

   (A) energy decreases  <-- correct
   (B) energy of the system does not change
   (C) energy increases
   (D) electron-electron repulsion becomes more than the nucleus-electron attraction

Answer: energy decreases
Explanation:
A chemical bond is a lasting attraction between atoms that enables the formation of chemical compounds. The bond may result from the electrostatic force of attraction between atoms with opposite charges, or through the sharing of electrons as in the covalent bonds. When a bond forms, electrons are attracted to the space between nuclei where the electrostatic force of attraction is greater. As the electrons fall to a position of lower potential energy, the total mechanical energy of the molecular system decreases. Part of the mechanical energy of the unbound atoms is lost when they form the molecular system with a lower total mechanical energy.

   (A) 3.72 $\times$ 10^(-20)Js
   (B) 8.68 $\times$ 10^(-20)Js  <-- correct
   (C) 5.72 $\times$ 10^(-20)Js
   (D) 7.72 $\times$ 10^(-20)Js

Answer: 8.68 $\times$ 10^(-20)Js
Explanation:
The energy of first (Bohr) orbit in hydrogen atom = -2.17 $\times$ 10^(-18)Jatom^(-1)

The energy of the fifth orbit will be given by $E_n= E_1\times {Z^2\over N^2}$ 

 $E_5={-2.17\times 10^{-18}\over 5^2}=$ 8.68 $\times$ 10^(-20)Jatom^(-1)

   (B) Both A and R are true but R is not the correct explanation of A.

   (D) A is true but R is false.

Answer: A is false but R is true.
Explanation:
Hybridization of C can be find out by counting $\sigma$ bonds and $\pi$ bonds present on C atom.

 $\underset{1\pi }{\overset{3\sigma }{\mathop{C{{H}_{2}}}}}\,=\underset{2\pi }{\overset{2\sigma }{\mathop{C}}}\,=\underset{1\pi }{\overset{3\sigma }{\mathop{C{{H}_{2}}}}}\,$ 

If C has 3 $\sigma$ bonds, it is sp^(2) hybridized. If C has 2 $\sigma$ bonds, it is sp hybridized.
