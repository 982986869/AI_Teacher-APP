Q1. Thermodynamics is the branch of physical science concerned with
    (A) heat and its transformations to and from other forms of energy.   <-- CORRECT
    (B) mass and its transformations to and from other forms of energy.
    (C) potential and its transformations to and from other forms of energy.
    (D) kinetic energy and its transformations to and from other forms of energy.
    Answer: A
    Explanation: Thermodynamics deals with heat and its transformation from one form to another. The branch of physical science that deals with the relations between heat and other forms of energy (such as mechanical, electrical, or chemical energy) and by extension of the relationships between all forms of energy.

Q2. In a closed system, which of the following take place?
    (A) The boundaries prevent the flow of matter out of it but not vice versa.
    (B) The boundaries permit the flow of matter into it but not vice versa.
    (C) The boundaries permit the flow of matter into or out of it.
    (D) The boundaries prevent the flow of matter into or out of it.   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: In a closed system, there is no flow of matter from system to surrounding or vice versa. For example, a certain quantity of fluid bounded within a closed cylinder constitutes a closed system.

Q3. In an open system, which of the following take place?
    (A) The boundaries prevent the flow of energy into it.
    (B) The boundaries prevent the flow of matter into or out of it.
    (C) The boundaries permit the flow of matter into or out of it.   <-- CORRECT
    (D) The boundaries prevent the flow of matter but not of energy.
    Answer: C
    Explanation: In an open system, there is a flow of matter and energy in and out of the system or vice versa. For example, an air compressor in which the air enters at low pressure and exit at high pressure and there is energy interaction with the surrounding.

    (A) law of conservation of energy.   <-- CORRECT
    (B) law of conservation of parity.
    (C) law of conservation of charge.
    (D) law of conservation of mass.
    Answer: A
    Explanation: It is a restatement of the law of conservation of energy.
It states that Thermodynamics is the branch of physical science that deals with the relations between heat and other forms of energy (such as mechanical, electrical, or chemical energy) and by extension, of the relationships between all forms of energy.

Q5. According to the first law of thermodynamics $\Delta$U = q + w, here what is a sign of q and w?
    (A) q is negative if heat is transferred from the system and w is negative if work is done by the system.
    (B) q is positive if heat is transferred into the system and w is positive if work is done on the system.   <-- CORRECT
    (C) q is negative if heat is transferred into the system and w is negative if work is done on the system.
    (D) q is positive if heat is transferred into the system and w is positive if work is done by the system.
    Answer: B
    Explanation: The first law is simply the conservation of energy equation. q is positive if heat is added to the system, and negative if heat is removed; w is positive if work is done on the system, and negative if work is done by the system.

Q1. A reaction, A + B $\to$ C + D + q is found to have a positive entropy change. The reaction will be:
    (A) nonspontaneous at all temperature.
    (B) spontaneous at all temperature.   <-- CORRECT
    (C) spontaneous only at low temperature.
    (D) spontaneous only at high temperature.
    Answer: B
    Explanation: $\Delta$G = $\Delta$H -T $\Delta$S
$\Delta$S is positive and $\Delta$H is negative as heat is liberated in the reaction.
So $\Delta$G is negative hence reaction will be spontaneous at all temperatures.

    (C) $\Delta {\text{U}}$ = 327 J
    (D) $\Delta {\text{U}}$ = 367 J
    Answer: B
    Explanation: $\Delta {\text{U}}$ = q + w (1st law of thermodynamics)
q = +701J (heat is absorbed by system)
w = -394 J (work is done by system)
$\Delta {\text{U}}$ = 701- 394
= 307J

    (A) 1 calorie $ \approx $ 3.2J
    (B) 1 calorie $ \approx $ 6.1J
    (C) 1 calorie $ \approx $ 5.2J
    (D) 1 calorie $ \approx $ 4.2J   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: Total work done on the water = w = - mg $\Delta$h = - (10) $\times$ (9.8) $\times$ (-3) = 294J
The heat required to increase the temperature of water by the same amount = q = mc $\Delta$T = 200 $\times$ 1 $\times$ 0.35 = 70cal
Because the work is done has the same effect on water as a direct transfer of heat, The two expression can be set equal to each other, giving
70cal = 294J
1 cal $\approx$ 4.2J

Q10. Suppose that 1.00 kJ of heat is transferred to 2.00 mol argon (at 298 K, 1 atm). What will the final temperature T_f, if the heat is transferred at constant volume?
    (A) 308 K
    (B) 347 K
    (C) 338 K   <-- CORRECT
    (D) 313 K
    Answer: C
    Explanation: Argon is monoatomic, Here, ${{C_v} = \frac{3}{2}R = \;\frac{3}{2} \times 8.314J{K^{ - 1}}mo{l^{ - 1}}}$ = 12.47J K^-1 mol^-1
As volume is kept constan t, q_v = nC_v $\Delta$T
$\Rightarrow$ 1000J = (2.00mol) $\times$ (12.47JK^-1 mol^-1) $\times$ $\Delta$T
$\Rightarrow $ $\Delta$ T = 40.09K
$\Rightarrow $ T_f = 298 + 40.1 = 338K
