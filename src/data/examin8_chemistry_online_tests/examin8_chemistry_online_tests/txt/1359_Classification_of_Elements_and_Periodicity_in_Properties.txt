Q4. Correct option to complete the following statement is:

    (A) molecular weights
    (B) atomic weight
    (C) atomic number
    (D) properties   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: Mendeleev believed that the properties of elements are the function of their atomic weights. He arranged elements in increasing order of their atomic weight but gave priority to the properties overweight. Wherever a dissimilarity in properties of an element occurred during arranging them in ascending order of their atomic weights, he ignored the atomic weight of the element but kept the elements with similar properties together.
Thus, he laid more stress over the similarities in the properties of different elements rather than their atomic weights.

Q7. It is now recognized that the ‘Modern Periodic Law’ is essentially the consequence of:

Q9. Ionization enthalpy increases across a period because:
    (A) outermost electrons are held more and more tightly.   <-- CORRECT
    (B) shielding is more effective.
    (C) screening is more effective.
    (D) outermost electrons are not held tightly.
    Answer: A
    Explanation: Consider moving from lithium to fluorine across the second period, where successive electrons are added to orbitals in the same principal quantum level. The shielding of the nuclear charge by the inner core of electrons does not increase very much to compensate for the increased attraction of the electron to the nucleus. Thus, across a period, increasing nuclear charge outweighs the shielding. Consequently, the outermost electrons are held more and more tightly and the ionization enthalpy increases across a period.

Q10. In the periodic table, Electronegativity generally:

    (B) decreases across a period from left to right and decreases down a group.
    (C) decreases across a period from left to right and increases down a group.
    (D) increases across a period from left to right and increases down a group.
    Answer: A
    Explanation: The electronegativity value increases as the effective nuclear charge on the atomic nucleus increases. In a period moving from left to right, the electronegativity increases due to a decrease in atomic radii resulting in an increase in effective nuclear charge. Whereas, in a group moving from top to bottom, the electronegativity decreases because of the atomic radius increases due to the subsequent addition of shells.
