    Explanation: $\frac{1}{2}$

$\alpha=\frac{-b+\sqrt{b^{2}-4 a c}}{2 a}$

ax^2 + bx + c = 0; a $\neq$ 0, we observe that the roots depend upon the value of the quantity b^2 - 4ac. This quantity is known as the discriminant of the quadratic equation and denoted by D.

Case III If b^2 - 4ac > 0 and it is not a perfect square, then roots are irrational and unequal.
Case IV If b^2 - 4ac < 0, then the roots are complex conjugate of each other.
