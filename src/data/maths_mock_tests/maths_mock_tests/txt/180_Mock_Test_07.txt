Q1. A - B is equal to
    (A) A^c $\cap$ B^c
    (B) A $\cap$ B
    (C) B - A
    (D) B^c - A^c   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: Let x $\in$ A - B $\Rightarrow$ x $\in$ A and x $\notin$ B
$\Rightarrow$ x $\notin$ A^c and x $\in$ B^c 
$\Rightarrow$ x $\in$ B^c and x $\notin$ A^c
$\Rightarrow$ x $\in$ B^c - A^c
$\Rightarrow$ A - B $\subset$ B^c - A^c .....(i)
Now let some y $\in$ B^c - A^c
$\Rightarrow$ x $\in$ B^c and x $\in$ A^c
$\Rightarrow$ x $\notin$ B and x $\in$ A
$\Rightarrow$ x $\in$ A and x $\notin$ B
$\Rightarrow$ x $\in$ A - B
$\Rightarrow$ B^c - A^c $\subset$ A - B ......(ii)
From (i) and (ii), we get A - B = B^c - A^c

Q3. If $\alpha, \beta$ are the roots of the equation ax^2 + bx + c = 0, then $\frac{1}{a \alpha+b}+\frac{1}{a \beta+b}$ =
    (A) $\frac{b}{ac}$   <-- CORRECT

    (D) $\frac{c}{ab}$

    Explanation: Given equation:
ax^2 + bx + c = 0

Then, sum of the roots = $\alpha+\beta$ = -$\frac{b}{a}$ 
Product of the roots = $\alpha \beta$ = $\frac{c}{a}$ 
$\therefore$ $\frac{1}{a \alpha+b}+\frac{1}{a \beta+b}$ = $\frac{a \beta+b+a \alpha+b}{(a \alpha+b)(a \beta+b)}$ 
= $\frac{a(\alpha+\beta)+2 b}{a^{2} \alpha \beta+a b \alpha+a b \beta+b^{2}}$ 
= $\frac{a(\alpha+\beta)+2 b}{a^{2} \alpha \beta+a b(\alpha+\beta)+b^{2}}$ 
= $\frac{a\left(-\frac{b}{a}\right)+2 b}{a^{2}\left(\frac{c}{a}\right)+a b\left(-\frac{b}{a}\right)+b^{2}}$ 
= $\frac{b}{ac}$

Q4. Sum of an infinite G.P. is $\frac 54$ times the sum of all the odd terms. The common ratio of the G.P. is
    (A) $\frac 1 3$
    (B) $\frac 1 4$   <-- CORRECT
    (C) 4
    (D) $\frac 2 3$
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Consider the infinite G.P a, ar, ar^2, ar^3,.... is a G.P with first term a and common ratio r
Then the odd terms a, ar^2, ar^4,.... is again a G.P with first term a and common ratio r^2 
We have $S_{\infty}=\frac{a}{1-r}$ 
Given $S_{\infty}=\frac{5}{4}$. Sum of odd terms
$\Rightarrow$ a + ar + ar^2 + ar^3 + .... = $\frac{5}{4}$[a + ar^2 + ar^4 + ...]
$\Rightarrow$ $\frac{a}{1-r}=\frac{5}{4} \cdot \frac{a}{1-r^{2}}$ 
$\Rightarrow$ $\frac{1}{1-r}=\frac{5}{4} \cdot \frac{a}{(1-r)(1+r)}$ 
$\Rightarrow$ 4(1 + r) = 5
$\Rightarrow$ 4r = 1
$\Rightarrow$ r = $\frac{1}{4}$
