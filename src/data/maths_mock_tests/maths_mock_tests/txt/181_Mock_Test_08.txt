Q6. $\lim \limits_{n \rightarrow \infty} \frac{1-2+3-4+5-6+\ldots +(2 n-1)-2 n}{\sqrt{n^{2}+1}+\sqrt{n^{2}-1}}$ is equal to
    (A) $-\frac{1}{2}$   <-- CORRECT
    (B) 1
    (C) -1
    (D) $\frac{1}{2}$
    Answer: A
    Explanation: $\lim \limits_{n \rightarrow \infty}\left[\frac{1-2+3-4+5-6+\ldots(2 n-1)-2 n}{\sqrt{n^{2}+1}+\sqrt{n^{2}-1}}\right]$
$=\lim \limits_{n \rightarrow \infty}\left[\frac{(1+3+5+\ldots 2 n-1)-(2+4+6+\ldots 2 n)}{(\sqrt{n^{2}+1}+\sqrt{n^{2}-1})}\right]$
$=\lim \limits_{n \rightarrow \infty}\left[\frac{\frac{n}{2}(1+2 n-1)-\frac{n}{2}(2+2 n)}{(\sqrt{n^{2}+1}+\sqrt{n^{2}-1})}\right]$
$=\lim \limits_{n \rightarrow \infty}\left[\frac{n^{2}-n(n+1)}{(\sqrt{n^{2}+1}+\sqrt{n^{2}-1})}\right]$
$=\lim \limits_{n \rightarrow \infty}\left[\frac{-n}{(\sqrt{n^{2}+1}+\sqrt{n^{2}-1})}\right]$
Dividing the numerator and the denominator by n
$=\lim \limits_{n \rightarrow \infty}\left[\frac{-1}{\sqrt{1+\frac{1}{n^{2}}}+\sqrt{1-\frac{1}{n^{2}}}}\right]$
$=\frac{-1}{1+1}$
$=\frac{-1}{2}$

    (C) $x-\sqrt{3} y-\sqrt{3}=0, x+\sqrt{3} y+\sqrt{3}=0$
    (D) $\sqrt{3} x+y+\sqrt{3}=0, \sqrt{3} x-y+\sqrt{3}=0$
    Answer: A
    Explanation: Suppose equation of any line passing through the point (1, 0) is
y – y_1 = m(x – x_1)
⇒ y – 0 = m(x – 1)
⇒ y = mx – m
⇒ mx – y – m = 0 …(i)

$\mathrm{d}=\left|\frac{\mathrm{Ax}_{1}+\mathrm{By}_{1}+\mathrm{C}}{\sqrt{\mathrm{A}^{2}+\mathrm{B}^{2}}}\right|$
$\Rightarrow \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}=\left|\frac{m \times 0+(-1) \times 0+(-m)}{\sqrt{(m)^{2}+(-1)^{2}}}\right|$
$\Rightarrow \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}=\left|\frac{-m}{\sqrt{m^{2}+1}}\right|$
$\Rightarrow \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}=\frac{\mathrm{m}}{\sqrt{\mathrm{m}^{2}+1}}$

$\Rightarrow \frac{3}{4}=\frac{m^{2}}{m^{2}+1}$
$\Rightarrow$ 3(m^2 + 1) = 4m^2

$\Rightarrow$ m^2 = 3

$\sqrt3$x – y – $\sqrt3$ = 0
Now, putting the value of m = -$\sqrt3$ in eq. (i), we get
-$\sqrt3$x – y – (-$\sqrt3$) = 0
$\Rightarrow$ -$\sqrt3$x – y + $\sqrt3$ = 0
