Q1. In a battle 70% of combatants lost one eye, 80% an ear, 75% an arm, 85% a leg, x% lose all the four limbs. The minimum value of x is:
    (A) 10   <-- CORRECT
    (B) 15
    (C) 12
    (D) 11
    Answer: A
    Explanation: Given that percentage of combatants who lost one eye = 70%, an ear = 80% and an arm = 75% and a leg = 85%
Hence we get the percentage of combatants who lost one eye and one ear = (70% + 80%) - 100% = 50%
Percentage of combatants who lost one eye, one ear and one arm = (50% + 75%) - 100% = 25%
Similarly, the percentage of combatants who lost one eye, one ear, one arm, and one leg = (25% + 85%) - 100% = 10%
Hence we get the percentage of the combatants who lost all 4 limbs = 10%
Hence if x% represents the percentage of combatants who lost all 4 limbs we get the minimum value of x = 10

Q3. If a cos $\theta$ + i sin $\theta$, then $\frac{1+a}{1-a}=$
    (A) cot $\theta$
    (B) $i \tan \frac{\theta}{2}$
    (C) $\cot \frac{\theta}{2}$
    (D) $i \cot \frac{\theta}{2}$   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: $i \cot \frac{\theta}{2}$
$a=\cos \theta+i \sin \theta$ (given)
$\Rightarrow \frac{1+a}{1-a}$ $=\frac{1+\cos \theta+i \sin \theta}{1-\cos \theta-i \sin \theta}$
$\Rightarrow \begin{array}{l} 1+a \\ 1-a \end{array}$ $=\begin{array}{l} 1+\cos \theta+i \sin \theta \\ 1-\cos \theta-i \sin \theta \end{array}$ $\times\begin{array}{l} 1-\cos \theta+i \sin \theta \\ 1-\cos \theta+i \sin \theta \end{array}$
$\Rightarrow \frac{1+a}{1-a}$ $=\frac{(1+i \sin \theta)^{2}-\cos ^{2} \theta}{(1-\cos \theta)^{2}-(i \sin \theta)^{2}}$
$\Rightarrow \frac{1+a}{1-a}$$=\frac{1-\sin ^{2} \theta+2 i \sin \theta-\cos ^{2} \theta}{1+\cos ^{2} \theta-2 \cos \theta+\sin ^{2} \theta}$
$\Rightarrow \frac{1+a}{1-a}$ $=\frac{1-\left(\sin ^{2} \theta+\cos ^{2} \theta\right)+2 i \sin \theta}{1+\left(\sin ^{2} \theta+\cos ^{2} \theta\right)-2 \cos \theta}$
$\Rightarrow \frac{1+a}{1-a}=\frac{2 i \sin \theta}{2(1-\cos \theta)}$
$\Rightarrow \frac{1+a}{1-a}=\frac{2 i \sin \frac{\theta}{2} \cos \frac{\theta}{2}}{2 \sin ^{2} \frac{\theta}{2}}$
$\Rightarrow \frac{1+a}{1-a}=\frac{i \cos \frac{\theta}{2}}{\sin \frac{\theta}{2}}$
$\Rightarrow \frac{1+a}{1-a}=i \cot \frac{\theta}{2}$

Q4. If $\frac{\left(a^{n+1}+b^{n+1}\right)}{\left(a^{n}+b^{n}\right)}$ is the arithmetic mean between unequal numbers a and b then n = ?
    (A) 1
    (B) 0   <-- CORRECT
    (C) 2
    (D) 4
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Here, it is given : $\frac{\left(a^{n+1}+b^{n+1}\right)}{\left(a^{n}+b^{n}\right)}=\frac{a+b}{2}$
$\Rightarrow \left(a^{n}+b^{n}\right)(a+b)=2\left(a^{n+1}+b^{n+1}\right)$
$\Rightarrow a^{n+1}+b^{n+1}+a^{n} b+b^{n} a=2 a^{n+1}+2 b^{n+1}$
$\Rightarrow a^{n+1}-a^{n} b+b^{n+1}-b^{n} a=0$
$\Rightarrow a^{n}(a-b)-b^{n}(a-b)=0$
$\Rightarrow (a-b)\left(a^{n}-b^{n}\right)=0 \Rightarrow a^{n}-b^{n}=0$ $[\because a-b \neq 0]$
$\Rightarrow$ a^n = b^n and $a \neq b \Rightarrow n=0$.

Q5. Two vertices of a triangle are (-2, -1) and (3, 2) and the third vertex lies on the line x + y = 5. If the area of the triangle is 4 square units, then the third vertex is
    (A) (0, 5) or (1, 4)
    (B) (0, 5) or (4, 1)   <-- CORRECT
    (C) (5, 0) or (4, 1)
    (D) (5, 0) or (1, 4)
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Let (h, k) be the third vertex of the triangle.
It is given that the area of the triangle with vertices (h, k), (-2, -1) and (3, 2) is 4 square units.
$\frac{1}{2}$[h(-1 - 2) - 3(-1 - k) - 2(2 - k)] = 4
$\Rightarrow$ 3h - 5k + 1 = $\pm$8
Taking positive sign, we get,
3h - 5k + 1 = 8
3h - 5k + 7 = 0 ...(i)
Taking negative sign, we get,
3h - 5k + 9 = 0 ...(ii)
The vertex (h, k) lies on the line x + y = 5.
h + k - 5 = 0 ...(iii)
On solving (i) and (iii), we find (4, 1) to be the coordinates of the third vertex.
Similarly, on solving (ii) and (iii), we find the required vertex (0, 5) or (4, 1).

    (C) symmetric relation only
    (D) an equivalence relation   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: Given Relation R = {(1, 1), (2, 2), (3, 3)}
Reflexive: If a relation has {(a, b)} as its element, then it should also have {(a, a), (b, b)} as its elements too.
Symmetric: If a relation has (a, b) as its element, then it should also have {(b, a)} as its element too.
