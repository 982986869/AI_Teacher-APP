Q2. Which is common in plant and animal cells?
    (A) Central vacuoles
    (B) Mitochondria   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Plastids
    (D) Centrioles
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Mitochondria are present in both plants as well as animal cells. Plastids and central vacuoles are present in plant cells and centrioles are present in only animal cells.

    (C) 36

    Explanation: 2n = 36
n = 18
The endosperm is triploid i.e. 3n = 3$\times$18 = 54.

Q14. Primary endosperm nucleus is formed by ________.
    (A) Egg synergid
    (B) Syngamy
    (C) Fusion of central nucleus
    (D) Triple fusion   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: The pollen tubes enter the embryo-sac where two male gametes are discharged. One of the male gametes fuses with the egg cell to form a zygote (syngamy). The other male gamete fuses with the diploid secondary nucleus to produce the triploid primary endosperm nucleus (PEN). Because of the involvement of two fusions, this event is termed as double fertilization.

    Answer: C
