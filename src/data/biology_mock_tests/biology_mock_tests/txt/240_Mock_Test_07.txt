Q11. A living organism is unexceptionally differentiated from a non-living structure on the basis of
    (A) reproduction.
    (B) growth and movement.
    (C) interaction with environment.
    (D) responsiveness.   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D

Q1. Assertion (A): Proteins are a heteropolymer.
Reason (R): Dietary proteins are the source of non-essential amino acids.
    (A) Both A and R are true and R is the correct explanation of A.
    (B) A is true but R is false.   <-- CORRECT
    (C) A is false but R is true.
    (D) Both A and R are true but R is not the correct explanation of A.
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Each individual protein is a polymer of amino acids. As there are 20 types of amino acids, a protein is a heteropolymer and not a homopolymer. Amino acids can be essential or non-essential. Certain amino acids are essential for our health and they have to be supplied through our diet. Dietary proteins are thus, a source of essential amino acids. Non-essential amino acids are those amino acids that are synthesized in our bodies.

    (C) Both A and R are true but R is not the correct explanation of A.   <-- CORRECT
    (D) A is false but R is true.
    Answer: C

Moreover constant agricultural cultivation in the field may also cause depletion of certain elements which must be replenished in order to improve fertility of soil. Fertilizers contain nitrogen, potassium and phosphorous which generally become deficient in soil. Thus fertilisers are used to increase crop yield. They have become water pollutants. Some of the fertilisers have reached ground water and cause malfunctioning in human beings, especially infants.

Q9. Which is not a function of the vacuole in a plant cell?
    (A) Cell elongation
    (B) Formation of H_2O_2   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Waste disposal
    (D) Storage
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Plant cell contains large central vacuole which helps in waste disposal, cell elongation and storage but not involved in the formation of H_2O_2.

Q10. The oils have:
    (A) Low melting point   <-- CORRECT
    (B) High melting point
    (C) Optimum melting point
    (D) No melting point
    Answer: A
    Explanation: Oils have a lower melting point (e.g., gingely oil) and hence remain as oil in winters.

Q11. The bacterial flagellum consists of
    (A) Filament ,basal body, pili
    (B) Filament, pili, and basal body
    (C) Filament, hook, and fimbriae
    (D) Filament, hook and basal body   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: A bacterial flagellum has 3 basic parts: a filament, a hook, and a basal body.
The filament is the rigid, helical structure that extends from the cell surface.
The hook is a flexible coupling between the filament and the basal body.
The basal body consists of a rod and a series of rings that anchor the flagellum to the cell wall and the cytoplasmic membrane.

Q12. The chemical compounds undergo changes in shape without the breakdown of bonds are called as:
    (A) Physical process   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Biological process
    (C) Inorganic chemical process
    (D) Chemical process
    Answer: A
    Explanation: Chemical compounds undergo two types of changes. A physical change simply refers to a change in shape without breaking of bonds. This is a physical process.
