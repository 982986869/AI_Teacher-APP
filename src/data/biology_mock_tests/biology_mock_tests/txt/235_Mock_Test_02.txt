    (B) Mitochondria   <-- CORRECT

    (D) Nucleus

Q1. Assertion (A): Enzymes lower the activation energy.
Reason (R): A substrate molecule can be acted upon by a particular enzyme.
    (A) Both A and R are true and R is the correct explanation of A.
    (B) A is true but R is false.
    (C) Both A and R are true but R is not the correct explanation of A.   <-- CORRECT
    (D) A is false but R is true.
    Answer: C
    Explanation: Activation energy is an external supply of energy that is needed for the initiation of the chemical reaction. The activation energy required for such a large number of reactions cannot be provided by living systems. Enzymes lower the activation energy required for a reaction. Enzymes are generally specific for their substrates.
