    (C) 5
    (D) 3

P(1) = 1 + 3
P(1) = 4

We need to check if P(k + 1) is divisible by 4
P(k+1) = (k + 1)^2 + 3(k + 1)
P(k+1) = k^2 + 1 + 2k + 3k + 3
P(k+1) = k^2 + 5k + 4
P(k+1) = (k^2 + 3k) + 2k + 4
P(k+1) = 4c + 2k + 4
P(k+1) = 4c + 2(k + 2)
Clearly the second part of the equation is not divisible by 4. However P(k) = 4c is divisible by 2 and
P(k + 1) is also divisible by 2. Therefore, 2 divides P(n).
