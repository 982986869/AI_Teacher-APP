Q1. If the line 2x – y + $\lambda $ = 0 is a diameter of the circle x^2 + y^2 + 6x - 6y + 5 = 0 then $\lambda $ =
    (A) 3
    (B) 6
    (C) 9   <-- CORRECT
    (D) 12
    Answer: C
    Explanation: Equation of circle is x^2 + y^2 + 6x - 6y + 5 = 0 
x^2 + 6x + y^2 - 6y = -5
Applying completing the square method
(x + 3)^2 + (y - 3)^2 = $(\sqrt{13})^{2}$ 
Comparing the above equation with (x - h)^2 + (y - k)^2 = (r)^2 we get center as (-3, 3) and radius as $\sqrt{13}$
As the centre of the circle lies on diameter, it will satisfy the equation of diameter, so on putting (-3, 3) in the equation of diameter we get 
2(-3) - (3) + $\lambda$ = 0
$\Rightarrow$ -6 - 3 - $\lambda$ = 0
$\Rightarrow$ $\lambda$ = 9
