Q5. The largest term in the expansion of (1 + x)^19 when x = $ \frac 1 2$ is
    (A) 5^th
    (B) 7^th   <-- CORRECT
    (C) 8^th
    (D) 6^th
    Answer: B
    Explanation: We have the general term in the expansion of (1 + x)^19 is given by 
T_r+1 = $^{19} C_{r}(x)^{r}$ 
Now $\frac{T_{r+1}}{T_{r}}=\frac{^{19} C_{r}}{_{19} C_{r-1}}(x)^{r}$ 
= $\frac{19 !(x)^{r}}{(19-r) ! \cdot r !} \times \frac{(20-r) !(r-1) !}{19 ! \cdot(x)^{r-1}}$ 
= $\frac{(20-r) x}{r}$ 
= $\frac{(20-r)}{2 r}$ since given x = $\frac{1}{2}$ 
Now $\frac{T_{r+1}}{T_{r}} \geq 1$ 
$\Rightarrow$ $\frac{(20-r)}{2 r} \geq 1$ 
$\Rightarrow$ 20 - r $\geq$ 2r
$\Rightarrow$ $r \leq \frac{20}{3}$ 
$\Rightarrow$ $r \leq 6 \frac{2}{3}$ 
Hence the maximum value of r is 6 and hence the greatest term is T_r+1 = T_7
