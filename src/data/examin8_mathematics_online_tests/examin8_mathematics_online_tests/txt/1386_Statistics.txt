    (A) 494

Q2. The S.D. of the observations 22, 26, 28, 20, 24, 30 is
    (A) 2.4
    (B) 2
    (C) 3
    (D) 3.42   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: $\bar X = \frac{(22 + 26 + 28 + 20 + 24 + 30)}{6}$ = 25
$\frac{1}{n}\sum\limits_{i = 1}^n {{{({X_i} - \bar X)}^2}} $ = $\frac{9 + 1 + 9 + 25 + 1 + 25}{6}$ = 11.66
S.D. = $ \sqrt {11.67} $ = 3.42

    (A) 5   <-- CORRECT
    (B) $\frac{-13}{2}$
    (C) 13
    (D) 11
    Answer: A
    Explanation: Mean = $\frac{{\frac{{n(n + 1)(2n + 1)}}{6}}}{n} = \frac{{(n + 1)(2n + 1)}}{6}$

$\Rightarrow$ 2n^2 + 13n - 10n - 65 = 0
$\Rightarrow$ (2n + 13)(n - 5) = 0
$\Rightarrow$ n = 5, $\frac{-13}{2}$

Q2. The mean of 50 observations is 36, if two observations are 30 and 42 are deleted, then the mean of the remaining observations is
    (A) 48
    (B) 36   <-- CORRECT
    (C) 46
    (D) 38
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Mean = $\frac{1}{n} \sum_{i=1}^{n} X_{i}$ 
$\Rightarrow$ 36 = $\frac{1}{50} \sum_{i=1}^{50} X_{i}$ 
$\Rightarrow$ $\sum_{i=1}^{50} X_{i}$ = 36 $\times$ 50 = 1800
After deleting the observations 30 and 42.
New sum = 1800 - 30 - 42 = 1728
New mean $\frac{1728}{48} $ = 36

Q5. The S.D. of the first n natural numbers is
    (A) $\sqrt{\frac{n^2-1}{12}}$   <-- CORRECT
    (B) $\frac{n + 1}{2}$
    (C) n
    (D) $\sqrt{\frac{n(n+1)}{2}}$
    Answer: A
    Explanation: First n natural numbers are 1, 2, 3.....n
We know that, $\sum x = \frac{{n(n + 1)}}{2}$ 
Sum of square is $\sum {{x^2}} = \sum {({1^2} + {2^2} + {3^2} + ......... + {n^2})} $ 
$\sum x^{2}=\frac{n(n+1)(2 n+1)}{6}$ 
SD = $\sqrt{\frac{\sum x^{2}}{n}-\left(\frac{\sum x}{n}\right)^{2}}$ 
= $\sqrt{\frac{n(n+1)(2 n+1)}{6 n}-\left(\frac{n(n+1)}{2 n}\right)^{2}}$ 
= $\sqrt{\frac{(n+1)(2 n+1)}{6}-\left(\frac{(n+1)}{2}\right)^{2}}$ 
= $\sqrt{(n+1)\left[\frac{2 n+1}{6}-\frac{(n+1)}{4}\right]}$ 
= $\sqrt{\frac{(n+1)(n-1)}{12}}$ 
= $\sqrt{\frac{n^{2}-1}{12}}$

    (A) 2 : 3

    (D) 3 : 2

    Explanation: Let the no. of boys be x and no. of girls be y
Sum of weights of boys = 80x
Sum of weights of girls = 50y
Sum of weights of boys and girls together = 60(x + y)
