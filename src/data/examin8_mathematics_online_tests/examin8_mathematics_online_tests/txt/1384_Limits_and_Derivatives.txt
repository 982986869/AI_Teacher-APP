Q1. $\mathop {Lt}\limits_{x \to 0} \frac{\log (1+a x)-\log (1+b x)}{x}$ is equal to
    (A) a – b   <-- CORRECT
    (B) log a + log b
    (C) log a – log b
    (D) a + b
    Answer: A
    Explanation: $\mathop {Lt}\limits_{x \to 0} \frac{\log (1+a x)}{x}-\mathop {Lt}\limits_{x \to 0} \frac{log (1 + bx)}{x}$ 
$\Rightarrow$ $\mathop {Lt}\limits_{x \to 0}\frac{\log (1+a x)}{x} \cdot \frac{a}{a}-\mathop {Lt}\limits_{x \to 0} \frac{\log (1+b x)}{x} \cdot \frac{b}{b}$ 
$\Rightarrow$ a - b

    (A) $x=2$ is the global maximum
    (B) $x=0$ is the global maximum
    (C) $x=1$ is the global maximum
    (D) $x=3$ is the global maximum   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: Clearly, $x=1,3$ are the points of global minimum (as the values being equal).
And, $x=0,4$ are the points of global maximum (as the values being equal).
And, $x=2$, is the point of local maximum (as the values being equal).
Thus, $x=3$ is the global maximum.

Q4. $\lim \limits_{x \to 0}\frac{\tan x-\sin x}{x^3}$ is equal to
    (A) 0
    (B) $\frac 1 2$   <-- CORRECT
    (C) 1
    (D) 4
    Answer: B
    Explanation: The function can be written as 
$\lim \limits_{x \rightarrow 0} \frac{\sin x(1-\cos x)}{\left(x^{3}\right)(\cos x)}$ 
$\Rightarrow$ $\lim _\limits {x \rightarrow 0} \left(\frac{\sin x}{x}\right) \cdot\left(\frac{1-\cos x}{x^2}\right) \cdot\left(\frac{1}{\cos x}\right)$ 
$\Rightarrow$ $1 \cdot \frac{1}{2} \cdot 1$ 
$\Rightarrow$ $\frac{1}{2}$

    (A) $ - \frac{1}{2}$   <-- CORRECT
    (B) $\frac{1}{2}$
    (C) 0
    (D) 1
    Answer: A
    Explanation: $\lim \limits_{n \rightarrow \infty}\left[\frac{1}{1-n^{2}}+\frac{2}{1-n^{2}}+ \ldots+\frac{n}{1-n^{2}}\right]$

= $\lim \limits_{n \rightarrow \infty}\left(\frac{1}{1-n^{2}}\right)\left[\frac{n(n+1)}{2}\right]$
= $\lim \limits_{n \rightarrow \infty} \frac{1}{2}\left[\frac{n(n+1)}{(1-n)(1+n)}\right]$
= $\lim \limits_{n \rightarrow \infty} \frac{1}{2} \frac{n}{1-n}$
= $\lim \limits_{n \rightarrow \infty} \frac{1}{2} \frac{1}{\frac{1}{n}-1}$
= $\frac{1}{2}$(-1)
= $\frac{-1}{2}$
