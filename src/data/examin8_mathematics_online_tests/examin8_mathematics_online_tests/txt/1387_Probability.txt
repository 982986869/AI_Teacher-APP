Q2. Two players toss four coins each. The probability that both obtain the same number of heads is
    (A) $\frac{5}{256}$
    (B) $\frac{1}{16}$
    (C) $\frac{35}{128}$   <-- CORRECT
    (D) $\frac{110}{256}$
    Answer: C
    Explanation: Let X denote the same number of heads Both will get.
So X can take values 
X = 0 ( both get 0 number of head)
X = 1 ( both get 1 head)
X = 2 ( both get 2 heads)
X = 3 ( both get 3 heads)
X = 4 ( both get 4 heads)
So, P(X = 0) = Probabilty of getting no heads = $\frac{1}{16} \times \frac{1}{16}$ = $\frac{1}{256}$ [by multiplication theorem P(X_i $ \cap $ X_j) = P(X_i).P(X_j) ]
P(X = 1) = Probabilty of getting 1 head = $\frac{4}{16} \times \frac{4}{16}$ = $\frac{16}{256}$
P(X = 2) = Probabilty of getting 2 heads = $\frac{6}{16} \times \frac{6}{16}$ = $\frac{36}{256}$
P (X = 3) = Probabilty of getting 3 heads = $\frac{4}{16} \times \frac{4}{16}$ = $\frac{16}{256}$
P(X = 4) = Probabilty of getting 4 heads = $\frac{1}{16} \times \frac{1}{16}$ = $\frac{1}{256}$
Therefore P (both get same number of head in a throw of 4 coins each)
= P(X = 0) + P(X = 1) + P(X = 2) + P(X = 3) + P(X = 4)
= $\frac{1}{256}$ + $\frac{16}{256}$ + $\frac{36}{256}$ + $\frac{16}{256}$ + $\frac{1}{256}$
= $\frac{70}{256}$ = $\frac{35}{128}$

Q12. A box contains n pairs of shoes and 2r shoes are selected (r < n).The probability that there is exactly one pair is
    (A) $\frac {(n.^{n-1}C_{r-1})2^{r-1}}{^{2n}C_{2r}}$   <-- CORRECT

    (D) $\frac{n.^{n-1}C_{r-1}}{^{2n}C_{2r}}$

    Explanation: The box contains 2n shoes. We can choose 2r shoes out of 2n shoes in $^{2n}C_{2r}$ ways. We can choose one complete pair out of n pairs in $^nC_1$ ways. Now, we have to avoid a complete pair. While choosing (2r-2)shoes out of remaining ( n-1)pairs of shoes, we first choose (r-1) pairs out of (n-1) pairs. This can be done in $^{n-1}C_{r-1}$ways. From each of these (r-1) pairs, choose (r-1) single (unmatching) shoes from each pair. This can be done in $2^{r-1}$ ways. Thus, the number of favourable ways is $(^nC_1)(^{n-1}C_{r-1}).2^{r-1}$.
Hence, the probability of the required event 
= $\frac{n(^{n-1}C_{r-1}).2^{r-1}}{^{2n}C_{2r}}$

Q1. If the integers m and n are chosen at random between 1 and 100, then the probability that a number of the form 7^m + 7^n is divisible by 5 is
    (A) $\frac{3}{4}$
    (B) $\frac{1}{7}$
    (C) $\frac{1}{5}$   <-- CORRECT
    (D) $\frac{1}{4}$
    Answer: C
    Explanation: We know that 7^k, K$\in$ N, has 1, 3, 9, 7 at the unit's place for k = 4p, 4p - 1, 4p - 2, 4p - 3 respectively, where p = 1, 2, 3,..... Clearly, 7^m + 7^n will be divisible by 5 if 7^m has 3 or 7 in the unit's place and 7^n has 7 or 3 in the unit's place or 7^m has 1 or 9 in the unit's place and 7^n has 9 or 1 in the unit's place.
Thus, for any choice of m,n the digit in the unit's place of 7^m = 7^n is 2, 4, 6, 0 or 8. It is divisible by 5 only when this digit is 0.
Hence, required probability = $\frac{1}{5}$

    Explanation: Five digits out of seven digits can be arranged in $^7P_5$ ways = 2520
There are 4 even numbers (2, 4, 6, 8).
Two can be arranged at the end in $^4P_2$ ways = 12
Remaining three places of five digit numbers can be filled with remaining 5 digits in $^5P_3$ ways = 60
So, required probability $=\frac{12×60}{2520}=\frac{2}{7}$

Q12. A sum of money is rounded of to the nearest rupee. The probability that the error occurred in rounding off is atleast 15 paise is
    (A) $\frac{71}{101}$
    (B) $\frac{29}{100}$
    (C) $\frac{71} {100}$   <-- CORRECT
    (D) $\frac{29}{101}$
    Answer: C
    Explanation: The sample space is, s = {-0.50, -0.49, -0.48, ......., -0.01, 0.00, 0.01, ....... 0.49}
Let A be the event that the round of error is at least 15 paise.
Then A^c is the event that the round of error is at most 14 paise.
The sample space of A^c is, 
A^c = {-0.14, -0.15, ........, -0.01, 0.00, 0.01, ........., 0.14}
$\therefore P({A^c}) = \frac{{29}}{{100}}$ 
$\therefore P(A) = 1 - p({A^c}) = 1 - \frac{{29}}{{100}} = \frac{{71}}{{100}}$

Q13. Three numbers are chosen at random from 1 to 20. The probability that they are consecutive is

Q15. A box contains 100 tickets numbered 1, 2, 3,........,100. Two tickets are chosen at random. If it is given that Maximum number on the two chosen tickets is not more than 10, then the probability that the minimum number on them is not less than 5 is,
    (A) $\frac{2}{3}$
    (B) $\frac{3}{5}$
    (C) $\frac{1}{3}$   <-- CORRECT
    (D) $\frac{2}{5}$
    Answer: C
    Explanation: Let A be the event, maximum number is not more than 10 and B be the event, minimum number is not less than 5
$\therefore P(A)=\frac{^{10}C_2}{^{100}C_2}\ and P(A \cap B)=\frac{^6C_2}{^{100}C_2}$ 
Required probability = P(B/A)$=\frac{P(A\cap B)}{P(B)}=\frac{^6C_2}{^{10}C_2}=\frac{1}{3}$
