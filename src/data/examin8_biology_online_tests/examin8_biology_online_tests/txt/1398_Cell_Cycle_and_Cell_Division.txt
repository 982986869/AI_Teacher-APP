Q1. Plants show mitotic divisions in ________.
    (A) Diploid cells
    (B) Haploid cells
    (C) Haploid cells and diploid cells   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Somatic cells
    Answer: C
    Explanation: The plants can show mitotic divisions in both haploid and diploid cells due to the alternation of generations in the life cycle.

Q2. What is the average cell cycle span for a mammalian cell?
    (A) 48 hours
    (B) 4 hours
    (C) 12 hours
    (D) 24 hours   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: All the phases of the cell cycle in the mammalian cell are completed in 24 hours.

Q3. The number of chromosomes and the amount of DNA are changed in:
    (A) Anaphase and S phase   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Interphase and S phase
    (C) Dilution and D phase
    (D) Metaphase and M phase
    Answer: A
    Explanation: S phase marks the period during which DNA synthesis or replication takes place. During this time the amount of DNA per cell doubles. If the initial amount of DNA is denoted as 2C then it increases to 4C and even in anaphase, the chromosome numbers are changed.

Q4. In yeast, the cell cycle is progressed through:
    (A) 60 minutes
    (B) 90 minutes   <-- CORRECT
    (C) 20 minutes
    (D) 30 minutes
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Yeast can progress through the cell cycle in only about 90 minutes due to its simple organisation.

Q5. M phase represents:
    (A) Mitosis phase   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Metaphase
    (C) Meiosis I phase
    (D) Meiosis phase
    Answer: A
    Explanation: The M Phase represents the phase when the actual cell division or mitosis occurs/starts during the cell cycle.

Q6. How many chromosomes are present in each cell of onion root tip?
    (A) 3 chromosomes
    (B) 4 chromosomes
    (C) 2 chromosomes
    (D) 16 chromosomes   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: Onion root tip cells have 16 chromosomes in each cell, the same as the number of chromosomes in somatic cells.

Q7. In animal cells, during G2 phase:
    (A) DNA are synthesized
    (B) Spindle fibres are formed
    (C) Middle lamellae are formed
    (D) Proteins are synthesized   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: During the G2 phase, proteins and RNA are synthesised in preparation for mitosis while cell growth continues.

Q8. The resting phase is otherwise called as______.
    (A) Metaphase
    (B) Telophase
    (C) Interphase   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Prophase
    Answer: C
    Explanation: The interphase though called the resting phase is the time during which the cell is preparing for division by undergoing cell growth in an orderly manner. No divisional activities occur during this phase. Hence the name.

Q9. In animal cells, the mitotic division is seen in:
    (A) Haploid somatic cells
    (B) Diploid somatic cells   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Haploid cells
    (D) Diploid cells
    Answer: B
    Explanation: In animals, mitotic cell division is only seen in the diploid somatic cell since mitosis produces cells with the same number of chromosomes as that of parents.

Q10. In which stage, the cells remain metabolically active but do not proliferate?
    (A) S-phase
    (B) G2-phase
    (C) G_o-phase   <-- CORRECT
    (D) G1-phase
    Answer: C
    Explanation: In the G_o stage, the cells are in a metabolically active state but do not divide unless there are some abnormal genes which reverse the same. This stage is also called the quiescent stage. Examples of human nerve cells.

Q11. S phase marks:
    (A) Chromosome condensation
    (B) DNA replication and chromosome duplication   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Cytoplasmic growth
    (D) It carries out normal metabolism
    Answer: B
    Explanation: S phase marks the phase of DNA replication and chromosome duplication. In this phase increase in the amount of DNA takes place. No synthesis of proteins or RNA takes place.

Q12. Which step proceeds reformation of the nuclear envelope during M-phase of the cell cycle?
 Decondenstion of chromosomes with the appearance of the nuclear lamina
 Transcription from chromosomes and reassemble of the nuclear lamina
 Formation of contractile ring and phragmoplast
 Formation of contractile ring and transcription
    (A) (A)   <-- CORRECT
    (B) (D)
    (C) (B)
    (D) (C)
    Answer: A
    Explanation: Before the completion of Mitosis reappearance of the nuclear membrane takes place along with the formation of nucleolus so that the cells become separate entities after cytokinesis but having the same chromosome number as parent cells.

Q13. The stage of inactivation of cell cycle due to non-availability of mitogens and energy-rich compounds is called:
    (A) S-phase
    (B) Interphase
    (C) M-phase
    (D) Quiescent phase   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: It is the stage of the cell cycle during which the cells remain metabolically active and do not divide due to the absence of mitogens and energy-producing compounds.

Q14. Karyokinesis is the:
    (A) Division of chromosome   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Equational division
    (C) Mitosis division
    (D) Division of cytoplasm
    Answer: A
    Explanation: Cell division consists of karyokinesis and cytokinesis. During karyokinesis division of chromosomes takes place and during cytokinesis division of cytoplasm takes place. Ultimately the cell divides into two halves.

Q15. Each chromosome consists of ________ chromatid.
    (A) two   <-- CORRECT
    (B) eight
    (C) four
    (D) one
    Answer: A
    Explanation: The resulting two identical copies of DNA are connected to each other at the centromere. Each copy is called a chromatid. A chromosome, before cell division is formed of one chromatid while it is formed of two chromatids after cell division.

    (A) Hormones and CDKs

 Prophase. The first and longest stage of mitosis.
 Metaphase. The second stage of mitosis.
 Anaphase. The third stage of mitosis.
 Telophase. The fourth and last stage of mitosis.

Electron micrographs of this stage indicate that chromosome synapsis is accompanied by the formation of complex structure called synaptonemal complex. The complex formed by a pair of synapsed homologous chromosomes is called a bivalent or a tetrad.

    Explanation: Egg yolk contains a lampbrush chromosome where two homologous chromosomes with several chiasmata with several loops in the chromatic region are found. They are also called giant chromosomes.

Q10. Number of chromosome and amount of DNA content per cell change during:
    (A) Telophase and M-phase
    (B) Anaphase and S-phase   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Interphase and G1 phase
    (D) Metaphase and S-phase
    Answer: B
    Explanation: The number of chromosome changes during anaphase of meiosis I and the amount of DNA content increases during S-phase of the cell cycle. This is reflected in the disturbance of the nucleocytoplasmic ratio.

Q11. The number of chromosomes and the amount of DNA are changed in:
    (A) Dilution and D phase
    (B) Metaphase and M phase
    (C) Interphase and S phase
    (D) Anaphase and S phase   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: S phase marks the period during which DNA synthesis or replication takes place. During this time the amount of DNA per cell doubles. If the initial amount of DNA is denoted as 2C then it increases to 4C and even in anaphase, the chromosome numbers are changed.

Q12. Each chromosome consists of ________ chromatid.
    (A) four
    (B) one
    (C) eight
    (D) two   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: The resulting two identical copies of DNA are connected to each other at the centromere. Each copy is called a chromatid. A chromosome, before cell division is formed of one chromatid while it is formed of two chromatids after cell division.

Q13. In the metaphase stage, the chromosomes are made up of:
    (A) One sister chromatid
    (B) Two sister chromatids   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Two non-sister chromatids
    (D) One sister and two non-sister chromatids
    Answer: B
    Explanation: At the metaphase stage, the chromosome is made up of two sister chromatids formed due to longitudinal splitting of the chromatids, which are held together by the centromere.

Q14. In plant cells, the new cell wall begins by the:
    (A) Chromatid
    (B) Cell plate   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Centromere
    (D) Cell cycle
    Answer: B
    Explanation: In-plant cells, wall formation starts in the centre of the cell and grows outward to meet the existing lateral walls. The formation of the new cell wall begins with the formation of a simple precursor, called the cell plate which hardens to give rise to the middle lamella.

Q15. Crossing over results in an exchange of genetic material, which occurs between:
    (A) Non-homologous chromosome
    (B) Sister chromatids
    (C) Bivalents
    (D) Homologous chromosomes   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: Crossing over is the exchange of genetic material between non-sister chromatids of two homologous chromosomes involved in the pairing process.
