Q1. The five kingdom classification was proposed by
    (A) Hutchinson
    (B) Whittaker   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Engler and Prantl
    (D) Bentham and Hooker
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Five kingdom classification was proposed by R.H. Whittaker in 1969. The kingdoms identified by him were Monera, Protista,Fungi, Plantae and Animalia.

Q2. Which among the five-kingdom system of classification do not possess nuclear membrane?
    (A) Kingdom Fungi
    (B) Kingdom Monera   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Kingdom Plantae
    (D) Kingdom Protista
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Kingdom Protista, kingdom Fungi, kingdom Plantae possess the nuclear membrane while the kingdom Monera does not possess a nuclear membrane.

Q3. In five kingdom of classification, Whittaker assigned eukaryotes to how many kingdoms?
    (A) Only three of five kingdoms
    (B) Only two of five kingdoms
    (C) Only four of five kingdoms   <-- CORRECT
    (D) All five kingdoms
    Answer: C
    Explanation: In Whittaker’s five kingdoms of classification, kingdom Protista, kingdom fungi, kingdom Plantae and kingdom Animalia are eukaryotic organisms. The kingdom monera includes prokaryotic organisms. Hence, four kingdoms out of five are eukayotes.

Q4. In five kingdom of classification, prokaryotic, non-nucleated and unicellular organisms are included under:
    (A) Kingdom Monera   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Kingdom Animalia
    (C) Kingdom Protista
    (D) Kingdom Plantae
    Answer: A
    Explanation: The fifth kingdom, Monera, contains all the single-celled organisms that do not possess a true nucleus. Members of the kingdom Monera are commonly referred to as bacteria.

Q5. Which kingdom brought Chlorella and Chlamydomonas with Paramoecium and Amoeba?
    (A) Kingdom Fungi
    (B) Kingdom Monera
    (C) Kingdom Plantae
    (D) Kingdom Protista   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: Kingdom Protista has brought together Chlamydomonas, Chlorella which were earlier placed in Algae within Plants and (both having cell walls) with Paramoecium and Amoeba which was earlier placed in the animal kingdom which lacks it.

Q6. In five kingdom of classification, the kingdom Plantae are autotrophic, eukaryotic and_______.
    (A) Motile
    (B) Possess sexual reproduction
    (C) All of these
    (D) Tissue level   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: The Kingdom Plantae includes all types of eukaryotic, multicellular, photosynthetic plants found in this biosphere. Most of the organism in this kingdom are autotrophs, which synthesis their own food with the help of solar energy.

Q7. Who was the earliest to attempt a more scientific basis for classification?
    (A) Pasteur
    (B) Aristotle   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Whittaker
    (D) Linnaeus
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Aristotle was the earliest to attempt a more scientific basis for classification. He used simple morphological characters to classify plants into trees, shrubs, and herbs.

Q8. Who divided animals into two groups based on the presence and absence of red blood cells?
    (A) Aristotle   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Linnaeus
    (C) Pasteur
    (D) Whittaker
    Answer: A
    Explanation: Aristotle used simple morphological characters to classify plants into trees, shrubs, and herbs. He also divided animals into two groups, those which had red blood and those that did not.

Q9. Which system of classification was developed during Linnaeus's time?
    (A) Five kingdom of classification
    (B) Three kingdom of classification
    (C) Four kingdom of classificaition
    (D) Two kingdom of classification   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: In Linnaeus' time, a two Kingdom system of classification with Plantae and Animalia kingdoms was developed that included all plants and animals respectively. This system was used until very recently.

Q10. Which of the following organisms were placed together under algae?
    (A) All of these   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Spirogyra
    (C) Chlorella
    (D) Chlamydomonas
    Answer: A
    Explanation: As spirogyra, chlorella and chlamydomonas have same characterstics which are perfectly suitable for the 'algae', they all are placed together in algae.

Q11. Who is called as Father of taxonomy?
    (A) Theophrastus
    (B) Linnaeus   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Aristotle
    (D) Hooker
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Carl Linnaeus, also known as Carl von Linné or Carolus Linnaeus, is often called the Father of Taxonomy. His system for naming, ranking, and classifying organisms is still in wide use today with many changes.

Q12. Which of the following type of classification based on a few morphological characters?
    (A) Phylogenetic
    (B) Artificial   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Natural
    (D) Morphological
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Artificial classification is based on a few morphological characters of organisms like flower shape, the stem type and types of seeds.

Q13. The first phylogenetic system of classification was given by:
    (A) Whittaker
    (B) John Ray
    (C) Engler and Prantl   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Bantham and Hooker
    Answer: C
    Explanation: The first phylogenetic system of classification was given by Engler and Prantl which was based on the evolutionary relationship of the organisms. Those evolved earlier are classified first and newly evolved later on.

Q14. Classification and studies based on chemical constituents of an organism are called _______.
    (A) Phytotaxonomy
    (B) Chemotaxonomy   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Cytotaxonomy
    (D) Karytaxonomy
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Chemotaxonomy as the method of biological classification based on similarities in the structure of certain compounds among the organisms being classified. Two organisms morphologically similar to each other are classified on the basis of chemotaxonomy.

Q15. Which one is not a characteristic feature of prokaryotes?
    (A) Sexual reproduction   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Absence of spindle apparatus
    (C) Genetic material not organized in nucleus
    (D) Single envelop organization
    Answer: A
    Explanation: Prokaryotic organisms generally do not reproduce by sexual reproduction. However, they reproduce by asexual methods in favorable conditions.

Q1. Arranging organism on the basis of their shared similar or derived characters that differ from ancestral character is called _________.
    (A) Cladogram   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Homogram
    (C) Histogram
    (D) Monogram
    Answer: A
    Explanation: A cladogram is arranging organisms on the basis of their shared similar or derived characters that differ from ancestral character. It is an important tool for taxonomical studies.

Q2. Unicellular algae and fungi are separated from other organisms on the basis of:
    (A) Presence of chlorophyll
    (B) Lack of tissue differentiation   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Absence of cilia
    (D) Absence of cell wall
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Unicellular organisms are made of only one cell and there is an absence of tissue differentiation. All unicellular algae and fungi are separated from other organisms on this basis.

Q3. Prokaryotes differ from Eukaryotes in the absence of:
    (A) Nuclear membrane and membrane bounded organelles   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Mitosis cell division and ribosome
    (C) All of these
    (D) Cell membrane and cytoplasm
    Answer: A
    Explanation: Prokaryotes differ from Eukaryotes in absence of nuclear membrane and membrane-bound organelles like mitochondria, Golgi bodies, etc.

Q4. Classification based on comparative cytological studies, the number of chromosome and behaviour of chromosome is called _______.
    (A) Chemotaxonomy
    (B) Cytotaxonomy   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Artificial taxonomy
    (D) Numerical taxonomy
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Cytotaxonomy is the branch of biology dealing with the relationships and classification of organisms using comparative studies of chromosomes.

Q5. Plant cells differ from animal cells in:
    (A) Having cell membrane and Golgi bodies
    (B) Having chloroplast and Golgi bodies
    (C) Having cell wall and Chloroplast   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Having chloroplast and Mitochondria
    Answer: C
    Explanation: Plant cells have a cell wall made up of cellulose and chloroplast that contain chlorophyll for photosynthesis. Animal cell lacks both cell wall and chloroplast.

Q6. A distinction between unicellular and multicellular is not possible in case of
    (A) Plantae
    (B) Animalia
    (C) Protozoa
    (D) Algae   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: Algae may be unicellular as well as multicellular, primitive algae are unicellular, and red and green algae are multicellular having a length of several meters. It is not possible to distinguish unicellular and multicellular algae because of this that unicellular green algae have not been included in kingdom Protista by Whittaker.

Q7. Which of the following archaebacteria is present in the guts of ruminant animals such as cows and buffaloes?
    (A) Speculums
    (B) Holophiles
    (C) Heterogens
    (D) Methanogens   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: Methanogens bacteria are present in the guts of ruminant animals such as cows and buffaloes. The characteristic smell of cow dungs is due to methane gas produced by this bacteria.

Q8. A peculiar odour found in marshy areas and cow sheds is of gas produced by________.
    (A) Cyanobacteria
    (B) Mycoplasm
    (C) Slime molds
    (D) Archaebacteria   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: The peculiar odour found in marshy areas and cow sheds is methane gas produced by methanogens bacteria that belong to class Archaebacteria.

Q9. The unicellular eukaryotes belong to the kingdom______.
    (A) Mycota
    (B) Plantae
    (C) Protista   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Monera
    Answer: C
    Explanation: Unicellular Eukaryotes were included in the kingdom Protista while unicellular Prokaryotes were included in the kingdom Monera.

Q10. A kingdom common to unicellular plants and animals is_______.
    (A) Protista   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Monera
    (C) Fungi
    (D) Algae
    Answer: A
    Explanation: Protista includes all unicellular prokaryotic organisms that include both plants and animals. So, Protista is common to unicellular plants and animals.

Q11. The infectious spore-like stage is seen in_______.
    (A) Flagellated protozoans
    (B) Sporozoans   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Amoeboid protozoans
    (D) Ciliated protozoans
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Sporozoans include diverse organisms that have an infectious spore-like stage in their life cycle.

Q12. In which group of kingdom Protista, plasmodium is formed?
    (A) Euglenoids
    (B) Dinoflagellates
    (C) Slime moulds   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Chrysophytes
    Answer: C
    Explanation: Slime moulds are saprophytic protists. The body moves along decaying twigs and leaves engulfing organic material. Under suitable conditions, they form an aggregation called plasmodium which may grow and spread over several feet. During unfavourable conditions, the plasmodium differentiates and forms fruiting bodies bearing spores at their tips.

Q13. Which criterion made Fungi to be placed in separate groups?
    (A) Cell wall   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Mode of nutrition
    (C) Nuclear membrane
    (D) Cell type
    Answer: A
    Explanation: The cell wall of fungi is made up of chitin while the green plants had a cellulosic cell wall. Due to this, fungi were placed in a separate kingdom – Kingdom Fungi.

Q14. How bacteria reproduce during unfavourable conditions?
    (A) By DNA transfer
    (B) By fission
    (C) By producing spores   <-- CORRECT
    (D) All of these
    Answer: C
    Explanation: Bacteria reproduce mainly by fission. Sometimes, under unfavourable conditions, they produce spores. They also reproduce by a sort of sexual reproduction by adopting a primitive type of DNA transfer from one bacterium to the other.

Q15. The filamentous body of the fungus is called _________.
    (A) Filament
    (B) Stipe
    (C) Hyphae
    (D) Mycelium   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: Mycelium is the vegetative part of a fungus or fungus-like bacterial colony, consisting of a mass of branching, thread-like hyphae.

Q1. All prokaryotic, unicellular organisms are included under the kingdom:
    (A) Monera   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Protista
    (C) Animalia
    (D) Fungi
    Answer: A
    Explanation: All prokaryotic, unicellular organisms are included in the kingdom Monera. The Eukaryotic unicellular organisms are placed in the Protista kingdom.

Q2. The cell wall of fungi consists of _______.
    (A) Starch
    (B) Glycopeptides
    (C) Cellulose
    (D) Chitin   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: The cell wall of fungi consists of special sugar molecules called chitin. The cell wall of plants is made up of cellulose.

Q3. Which among the following is photosynthetic as well as saprophytic?
    (A) Mushroom
    (B) Euglena   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Paramoecium
    (D) Amoeba
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Euglena is photosynthetic in presence of sunlight and is saprophytic in absence of sunlight.

Q4. Which bacteria help in oxidising inorganic substances?
    (A) Autotrophic bacteria
    (B) Chemosynthetic autotrophic bacteria   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Cyanobacteria
    (D) Heterotrophic bacteria
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Chemosynthetic autotrophic bacteria oxidize various inorganic substances such as nitrates, nitrites, and ammonia and use the released energy for their ATP production. They play a great role in recycling nutrients like nitrogen, phosphorous, iron, and sulphur.

Q5. The asexual reproduction in fungi takes place by
    (A) Ascospores
    (B) Basidiospores
    (C) OOspores
    (D) Zoospores   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: In fungi, the asexual reproduction takes place by spores called conidia or sporangiospores or zoospores, and sexual reproduction is by oospores, ascospores and basidiospores.

Q6. A distinction between unicellular and multicellular is not possible in________.
    (A) Plantae
    (B) Algae   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Animalia
    (D) Protozoa
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Algae grouped together the unicellular organisms and the multicellular ones.

Q7. Which among the following is not included in the three domains system?
    (A) Viral Domain   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Bacteria Domain
    (C) Eukara Domain
    (D) Archaea Domain
    Answer: A
    Explanation: The three-domain system includes Archaebacteria, Eubacteria, and Bacteria domain.

Q8. Which archaebacteria is present in guts of ruminant animals such as cows and buffaloes?
    (A) Methanogens   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Heterogens
    (C) Thermoacidophiles
    (D) Holophiles
    Answer: A
    Explanation: Methanogens are present in the guts of several ruminant animals such as cows and buffaloes and they are responsible for the production of methane (biogas) from the dung of these animals.

Q9. The blue-green algae are also referred to as________.
    (A) Heterotrophic bacteria
    (B) Archaebacteria
    (C) Eubacteria
    (D) Cyanobacteria   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: Blue-green algae are also called cyanobacteria. They are considered as the most primitive organism that still survives. It includes different forms of unicellular and multicellular organisms.

Q10. The organism that completely lacks a cell wall and is the smallest living cell known that can highly pleomorphic is _________.
    (A) Bacteriophages
    (B) Yeast
    (C) Virus
    (D) Mycoplasma   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: Mycoplasma is a genus of bacteria that lack a cell wall around their cell membrane. Without a cell wall, they are unaffected by many common antibiotics such as penicillin or other beta-lactam antibiotics that target cell wall synthesis.
Mycoplasma species are the smallest bacterial cells yet discovered, can survive without oxygen, and come in various shapes. For example, M. genitalium is flask-shaped.

Q11. Which one is not a member of the kingdom Protista?
    (A) Slime molds
    (B) Protozoa
    (C) Phycomycetes   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Euglenoids
    Answer: C
    Explanation: Phycomycetes is a subdivision of fungus which are multicellular. Protozoa, slime moulds and euglenoids are part of the kingdom Protista which are unicellular and eukaryotic organisms.

Q12. The class of fungi in which mushroom is included is________.
    (A) Ascomycetes
    (B) Deuteromycetes
    (C) Basidiomycetes   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Phycomycetes

    Explanation: Commonly known forms of basidiomycetes are mushrooms, bracket fungi, or puffballs.

Q13. Diatoms do not decay easily because:
    (A) They have siliceous walls   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Their body is impervious to water
    (C) They are chitinous
    (D) They are abundant in saline water

    Explanation: The walls of the diatoms are embedded with silica and thus the walls are indestructible.

Q14. In fungi like ascomycetes and basidiomycetes, an intervening dikaryotic stage occurs. Such condition is called
    (A) Mycorrhizae
    (B) Karyogamy
    (C) Plasmogamy
    (D) Dikaryon   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: In fungi like ascomycetes and basidiomycetes, an intervening dikaryotic stage (n + n i.e. two nuclei per cell) occurs; such a condition is called a dikaryon and the phase is called dikaryon phase of fungus.

Q15. The body of the fungus is filamentous and it is called _______.
    (A) Hyphae
    (B) Filament
    (C) Mycelium   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Steepened
    Answer: C
    Explanation: The body of the filamentous fungus is known as mycelium, which is a thread-like structure. Mycelium produces hyphae and sporogenous cells to produce spores.

    (A) Protozoa
    (B) Amphibians
    (C) Algae
    (D) Fungi   <-- CORRECT

    (A) Phosphoglceride
    (B) Glycogen and fat   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Lipid
    (D) Glucose and fat
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Different fungi contain different kinds of reserve food materials depending upon the habitat and body structure of the fungus. In some fungi, the food reserves are glycogen and fat.

Q4. In which class of fungi the mushroom belongs to:
    (A) Ascomycetes
    (B) Phycomycetes
    (C) Basidiomycetes   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Deuteromycetes
    Answer: C
    Explanation: The mushroom belongs to class Basidiomycetes to kingdom fungi. Basidiomycetes produce basidiospores as reproductive units. The member of this group grows on dead and decaying matters.

Q5. Which is the possible region of respiration in bacteria?
    (A) Cell wall
    (B) Mesosome   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Mitochondria
    (D) Nucleoid
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Bacteria belong to the kingdom Monera in which organisms are unicellular and prokaryotic. Prokaryotic organisms lack membrane-bounded organelles like mitochondria where respiration occurs. In bacteria, respiration occurs in the mesosome.

    (A) Endotoxins
    (B) Thick wall
    (C) Endospore   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Endogenous buds

Q7. When a freshwater protozoan is placed in marine water:
    (A) Number of contractile vacuole increases
    (B) The contractile vacuole increase in size
    (C) The contractile vacuole remain same
    (D) The contractile vacuole disappear   <-- CORRECT

    Explanation: When freshwater protozoan is placed in marine water, the contractile vacuole disappears because the function of the vacuole is to expel out excess water but in the case of marine water, the water should be retained instead of getting expelled. If the contractile vacuole will not disappear the cell will get dehydrated.

    (A) Alternation of diploid and haploid

    (A) Fungi

    (D) Orange

    Explanation: Mycophages are not a group of bacteria. They are a group of organisms that use algae as food. The animal virus is called Zoophagineae, plant virus phytophagineae, and the bacterial virus is called bacteriophages.

Q12. Two categories of animals on the basis of red blood are:
    (A) Animate and inanimate
    (B) Enaima and Anaima   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Erythrocytes and Thrombocytes
    (D) Leucocytes and erythrocytes
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Haemoglobin in RBCs imparts red colour to it. The organisms whose blood contains haemoglobin are called enaima and those without it are called anaima.

Q13. The fusion of protoplasm between to motile or non-motile gamete is called ________.
    (A) Plasmogamy   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Karyogamy
    (C) Homogamy
    (D) Heterogamy
    Answer: A
    Explanation: Plasmogamy is the fusion of protoplasm of two cells which may be motile or non- motile. Plasmogamy occurs during sexual reproduction to form a diploid organism.
