Q1. The chief pigment associated in the process of photosynthesis is:
    (A) Chlorophyll m
    (B) Chlorophyll a   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Chlorophyll c
    (D) Chlorophyll b
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Chlorophyll-a is the chief pigment associated with photosynthesis because, in the blue and red regions of the spectrum, there is maximum absorption by chlorophyll-a. It also shows a higher rate of photosynthesis.

Q2. The essential role of air in the growth of green plants is given by:
    (A) Julis von sach
    (B) Joseph Priestly   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Cornelius van niel
    (D) Engelmann
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Joseph Priestley (1733-1804) in 1770 performed a series of experiments that revealed the essential role of air in the growth of green plants.

Q3. The site in chloroplast which is responsible for trapping for light energy is:
    (A) Stromal lamellae
    (B) Stroma
    (C) Grana   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Ribosomes
    Answer: C
    Explanation: The grana are responsible for trapping the light energy and converting it into ATP and NADPH during the light reaction of photosynthesis.

Q4. Photosynthesis mainly take place in:
    (A) Flowers
    (B) Roots
    (C) Leaves   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Stem
    Answer: C
    Explanation: Photosynthesis takes place only in the green parts of the plants, mainly the leaves.

Q5. Photosynthesis is maximum in:
    (A) Red light   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Very high light
    (C) Green light
    (D) Continuous light
    Answer: A
    Explanation: There is maximum absorption by chlorophyll a, in the red region and so there is a higher rate of photosynthesis.

Q6. In Bacterial photosynthesis, hydrogen donor is:
    (A) O_2
    (B) H_2O
    (C) H_2S   <-- CORRECT
    (D) CO_2
    Answer: C
    Explanation: In green plants, H_2O is the hydrogen donor and is oxidised to O_2. Some organisms do not release O_2 during photosynthesis. When H_2S, instead is the hydrogen donor for purple and green sulphur bacteria, the ‘oxidation’ product is sulphur or sulphate depending on the organism.

Q7. Solarisation is:
    (A) Effect of solar light
    (B) Formation of chlorophyll
    (C) Destruction of chlorophyll   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Utilisation of sunlight
    Answer: C
    Explanation: Solarisation is the inhibition of photosynthesis at very high light intensities due to photooxidation of certain compounds and the destruction of chlorophyll molecules.

Q8. The substances that have the ability to absorb light at different specific wavelength are:
    (A) Proteins
    (B) Enzymes
    (C) Pigments   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Carbohydrates
    Answer: C
    Explanation: Pigments are substances that have an ability to absorb light, at specific wavelengths. The chlorophyll-a pigment absorbs lights of different wavelengths.

Q9. The splitting of water molecule take place inside:
    (A) Outer membrane of thylakoid
    (B) Lumen of thylakoid
    (C) Stroma
    (D) Inner membrane of thylakoid   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: Splitting of the water molecule takes place on the inner side of the membrane, the protons or hydrogen ions that are produced by the splitting of water accumulate within the lumen of the thylakoids.

Q10. PGA as the first CO_2 fixation product was discovered in photosynthesis of:
    (A) Algae   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Gymnosperm
    (C) Bryophytes
    (D) Angiosperm
    Answer: A
    Explanation: The pathway of photosynthesis was discovered by Melvin Calvin after the experiment on single-celled algae. The first stable product of the C_3 cycle is a three-carbon compound called PGA (phosphoglyceric acid).

Q11. Melvin Calvin use the radioactive ^14C in:
    (A) All of these
    (B) Plant photosynthesis
    (C) Bacterial photosynthesis
    (D) Algal photosynthesis   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: The use of radioactive C-14 by him in algal photosynthesis studies led to the discovery that the first CO_2 fixation product was a 3-carbon organic acid.

Q12. Accessory pigments protect plants from:
    (A) Photosystem
    (B) Photo-oxidation   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Photosynthesis
    (D) Photorespiration
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Other thylakoid pigments like chlorophyll b, xanthophylls, and carotenoids, which are called accessory pigments, also absorb light and transfer the energy to chlorophyll a. Indeed, they not only enable a wider range of wavelengths of incoming light to be utilized for photosynthesis but also protect chlorophyll from photo-oxidation.

Q13. The portion of ATPase enzyme that protrudes on the outer surface of the thylakoid membrane on the side faces of the stroma _______.
    (A) ATP
    (B) F_0 of the ATPase
    (C) NADH
    (D) F_1 of the ATPase   <-- CORRECT

The other portion is called F_1 and protrudes on the outer surface of the thylakoid membrane on the side that faces the stroma.

Q14. Which form of carbon is absorbed by the hydrophytes through their general body surface?
    (A) Bicarbonates   <-- CORRECT

The external factors would include the availability of sunlight temperature, CO_2 concentration, and water. As a plant photosynthesizes, all these factors will simultaneously affect its rate.

Q12. Half leaf experiment is performed to prove ________ is required for photosynthesis.
    (A) CO_2   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Light
    (C) Chlorophyll
    (D) Oxygen
    Answer: A
    Explanation: Moll's half leaf experiment is done to show that carbon dioxide is necessary for photosynthesis to occur. In this experiment Moll (inventor of an experiment) divided the leaf into two half portion one inside the bottle and another outside. The one inside the bottle did not show photosynthesis due to a lack of carbon dioxide. After testing leaf present inside the bottle for starch, it is proved that it does not contain starch and hence, photosynthesis did not occur in this leaf. The leaf which was outside the bottle shows the presence of starch indicating the occurrence of photosynthesis in it.

    (D) About 70%

    Explanation: Solar radiation consists of light of different wavelengths ranging from ultraviolet to infrared. A small portion of these light is useful for photosynthesis called PAR or photosynthetically active radiation which comprises less than 50% of total radiation.

Q14. Passage of light rays in between the chloroplasts of palisade parenchyma cells to spongy parenchyma is known as:
    (A) Sieve Effect   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Hydro Effect
    (C) Light Effect
    (D) Salt Effect
    Answer: A
    Explanation: The passage of light rays in between the chloroplast of palisade parenchyma cells to spongy parenchyma is known as the Sieve effect because the light that falls on chloroplast will be absorbed and reflected back. the light that passes between the chloroplast will reach the spongy parenchyma.
