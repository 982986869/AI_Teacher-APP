Q1. Which phylum contains coelomic and triploblastic animals?
    (A) Phylum Platyhelminthes   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Phylum Porifera
    (C) Phylum Ctenophora
    (D) Phylum Coelenterata
    Answer: A
    Explanation: Those animals in which the developing embryo has a three germinal layer, mesoderm in between the ectoderm and endoderm, are called triploblastic animals, and the absence of an internal body cavity is called coelomic.

Q2. Choose the odd one out based on coelom: Annelids, Molluscs, Platyhelminthes, Arthropods.
    (A) Platyhelminthes   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Molluscs
    (C) Annelids
    (D) Arthopods
    Answer: A
    Explanation: Animals possessing coelom are called coelomates such as annelids, molluscs, arthropods, echinoderms, hemichordates and chordates. In Platyhelmites coelom is absent.

Q3. In Coelenterates, the cell shows:
    (A) Organ system level of organisation
    (B) Organ level of organisation
    (C) Tissue level of organisation   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Cellular level of organisation
    Answer: C
    Explanation: In coelenterates, the arrangement of cells is more complex. Here, the cells performing the same function are arranged into tissues, hence is called tissue level of organization.

Q4. The cross-section of the body of an invertebrate is given below. Identify the animal which has this body plan:-
    (A) Cockroach
    (B) Earthworm
    (C) Round worm   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Planaria
    Answer: C
    Explanation: In some animals, the body cavity is not lined by mesoderm, instead, the mesoderm is present as scattered pouches in between the ectoderm and endoderm. Such a body cavity is called pseudocoelom and the animals possessing them are called pseudocoelomates, e.g.,aschelminthes such as round worm.

Q5. The largest phylum of kingdom animalia is ________.
    (A) Nematode
    (B) Arthropoda   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Chordate
    (D) Annelida
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Arthropoda is the largest phylum of kingdom Animalia. It includes all insects, arachnids, myriapods, and crustaceans. They are characterized by jointed limbs and cuticles.

Q6. Egg of reptiles and birds are:
    (A) Mesolecithal

    (C) Polylecithal   <-- CORRECT

    Explanation: The eggs of reptiles and birds are polyecithal because they contain a large amount of yolk inside the egg.

    (D) Spongilla   <-- CORRECT

Sponges of the genus Spongilla attach themselves to rocks and logs and filter the water for various small aquatic organisms such as protozoans, bacteria, and other free-floating pond life. Unlike marine sponges, freshwater sponges are exposed to far more adverse and variable environmental conditions, so they have developed gemmules as a means of dormancy.
