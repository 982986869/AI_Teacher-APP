Q1. In Phaeophyceae, the food stored in the form of:
    (A) Protein
    (B) Mannitol, laminarin   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Oil droplets
    (D) Starch
    Answer: B
    Explanation: In Phaeophyceae, food is stored as complex carbohydrates which may be in the form of laminarin or mannitol.

Q2. Kelps are ________.
    (A) Fresh water algae
    (B) Marine algae   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Terrestrial plant
    (D) Amphibious plant
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Kelps are marine algae of class Phaeophyceae or brown algae. The body of kelp may reach up to 100 meters long. They vary in colour from olive green to various shades of brown depending upon xanthophyll pigments, fucoxanthin present in them.

Q3. An algae having ribbon-shaped chloroplast is_______.
    (A) Kelps
    (B) Chara
    (C) Ulothrix
    (D) Spirogyra   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: The shape of a chloroplast varies from species to species. It may be cup-shaped (e.g., Chlamydomonas), (e.g., Vaucheria), Girdle (e.g., Ulothrix), Stellate or Star-shaped (e.g., Zygnema), Reticulate or net-like (e.g., Cladophora, Oedogonium), Spiral or ribbon or scalariform (e.g., Spirogyra), ovoid or disc or spheroid in higher plants.

Q4. Laminaria and Fucus belong to:
    (A) Brown algae   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Golden brown algae
    (C) Green algae
    (D) Red algae
    Answer: A
    Explanation: The common forms of Brown algae or Phaeophyceae are Ectocarpus, Dictyota, Laminaria, Sargassum and Fucus.

Q5. Angiosperm: roots :: Phaeophyceae:
    (A) Holdfast   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Root hair
    (C) Prop root
    (D) Substratum
    Answer: A
    Explanation: The plant body of Phaeophyceae is usually attached to the substratum by a holdfast whereas the angiosperms attach to the substratum with the help of roots.

Q6. Half of the total carbon dioxide fixation on earth is carried out by algae through ________.
    (A) Absorption
    (B) Transportation
    (C) Respiration
    (D) Photosynthesis   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: Being photosynthetic, algae increase the level of dissolved oxygen in their immediate environment.

Q7. In Ulothrix or Spirogyra, reduction division (meiosis) occurs at the time of:
    (A) Gamete formation
    (B) Vegetative reproduction
    (C) Zygospore germination   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Zoospore formation
    Answer: C
    Explanation: In Ulothrix/Spirogyra reduction division (meiosis) occurs at the time of zygospore germination. The plant body of Ulothrix and Spirogyra is gametophytic (haploid), they produce zoogametes (n) which fuses to form zygospores (2n) diploid, which is a resting spore. The onset of favourable condition zygospore undergoes reductional division, or meiosis to produce zoo- meiospores.

Q8. The cell wall of green algae is made up of _______.
    (A) Glycoproteins
    (B) Hemicellulose
    (C) Cellulose   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Proteins
    Answer: C
    Explanation: Green algae usually have a rigid cell wall made of an inner layer of cellulose and an outer layer of pectose.

Q9. Which classes of algae possess chlorophyll a, chlorophyll b, carotenoids and xanthophylls?

Q3. Which of the following is responsible for peat formation ?
    (A) Marchantia
    (B) Riccia
    (C) Funaria
    (D) Sphagnum   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: Sphagnum mosses not only dominate large areas of the boreal peatlands, but they have also formed these habitats by their growth and production of decay-resistant litter. As they invade, they can quickly transform a high-pH fen with little peat accumulation to a bog where large amounts of carbon are stored. They are also responsible for the many types of surface patterns observed in peatlands. Their ability to shape and transform mires can to a large extent be understood from the peculiar anatomy and biochemistry, a fact which makes the links between species’ traits and ecosystem functions in peatlands very strong.

Q4. Which of the following is produced by moss spore on germination?
    (A) Protonema   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Annulus
    (C) Theca
    (D) Peristome
    Answer: A
    Explanation: The spore of moss germinate to produce a protonema, which is a green, branched, and frequently filamentous stage and develops a lateral bud which is called secondary protonema or a leafy stage. Secondary protonema also gives rise to gametophore initials. Secondary protonema can undergo both sexual and asexual modes of reproduction. It reproduces asexually by the processes of budding and fragmentation. Sexual reproduction is the process that occurs in the leafy stage.

Q5. Asexual reproduction in liverworts takes place by specialised structures, commonly known as:
    (A) Fragmentation
    (B) Budding
    (C) Gemmae cups   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Spores
    Answer: C
    Explanation: Asexual reproduction in liverworts takes place by the formation of specialized structures called gemmae (sing. gemma). Gemmae are green, multicellular, asexual buds, which develop in small receptacles called gemma cups located on the thalli. The gemmae become detached from the parent body and germinate to form new individuals.

    (D) Gymnosperms   <-- CORRECT

The gymnosperms (Gymnos: naked, Sperma: seeds) are planted in which the ovules are not enclosed by any ovary wall and remain exposed, both before and after fertilization. The seeds that develop post-fertilization, are not covered, i.e., are naked.

    (D) Flagellate antheriozoids
    Answer: B
    Explanation: In bryophytes, the dominant phase in the life cycle is the gametophytic plant body. However, in pteridophytes, the main plant body is a sporophyte which is differentiated into true root, stem and leaves.

Q6. All seed-bearing plant, alternation of generation is_________.
    (A) Haplo-diplontic
    (B) Monoplontic
    (C) Haplontic
    (D) Diplontic   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: All seed-bearing plants i.e., gymnosperms and angiosperms follow diplontic alternation of a generation where the diploid sporophyte is the dominant, photosynthetic, independent phase of the plant. The gametophytic phase is represented by the single to the few-celled haploid gametophyte.

Q7. Megasporangium of seed plants are called:
    (A) Ovary
    (B) Anther
    (C) Thalamus
    (D) Ovule   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: Megasporangium of seed plants is called an ovule. The ovule is present inside the ovary which becomes seed on maturation after fertilization. It produces megasporocytes or Megaspore mother cells that yield megaspores.

Q8. Angiosperm: roots :: Rhodophyta :?
    (A) Holdfast   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Root hair
    (C) Prop root
    (D) Substratum
    Answer: A
    Explanation: Angiosperms plants have roots to hold the soil and remain erect. Similarly, Rhodophytes have holdfast by which they remain attached with substratum to absorb water and minerals.

Q9. Primary endosperm nucleus is formed by _____________.
    (A) Egg synergids
    (B) Fusion of central nucleus
    (C) Triple fusion   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Syngamy
    Answer: C
    Explanation: The fusion of two polar nuclei with one male gamete is called triple fusion. The primary endosperm nucleus is formed by triple fusion and divide mitotically to form endosperm.

Q10. The phloem of angiosperms differ from that of other vascular plants by the presence of:
    (A) Vessels
    (B) Trachieds
    (C) Companion cells   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Albuminous cells
    Answer: C
    Explanation: Gymnosperms and lower vascular plants have sieve cells as their sieve elements, while sieve tubes and companion cells are characteristic of flowering plants (angiosperms). Phloem transports food from leaves to all other parts of plants.

Q11. Some plants are dependent on the larger plant for space only. Such plants are called as ________.
    (A) Halophytes
    (B) Mesophytes
    (C) Epiphytes   <-- CORRECT
    (D) bryophyte
    Answer: C
    Explanation: Epiphytes are dependent on other large plants for space or physical support. It remains to attach with other plants but does not obtain food or another nutrient from it so they are not parasitic on the supporting plants.

Q12. Which of the following includes a diploid structure?
    (A) Gametophyte and spore mass
    (B) Gametophyte and Capsule   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Gametophyte and oospores
    (D) Gametophyte and antheridia
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Gametophytes and capsule are diploid structure and alternate with a haploid structure called spores by reduction division.

Q13. Non-flowering plants belong to:
    (A) Monocots
    (B) Phanerogams
    (C) Cryptogams   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Dicots
    Answer: C
    Explanation: The cryptogams are flowerless and seedless plants. They are simple plants like algae, mosses, and ferns which do not produce flowers, fruits, and seeds. Cryptogams are considered as lower plants.

Q14. In which one of the following, male and female gametophytes do not have free living independent existence?
    (A) Pteris
    (B) Cedrus   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Funaria
    (D) Polytrichum
    Answer: B
    Explanation: In Cedrus plants, male and female gametophytes do not have a free independent existence. They remain attached to sporophytic forms to obtain nutrients.

Q15. Fusion between one large and non-motile female gamete with smaller, motile male gamete is termed as______.
    (A) Homogamous
    (B) Oogamous   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Isogamous
    (D) Anisogamous
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Oogamy is a form of anisogamy (heterogamy) in which the female gamete (e.g. egg cell) is significantly larger than the male gamete and is non-motile.
