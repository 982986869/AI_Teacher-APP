Q1. The oils have:

Q2. The diagram represents
    (A) Nucleotide
    (B) Lipids
    (C) Nitrogen base   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Nucleoside

    (A) 20 carbon atoms   <-- CORRECT
    (B) 16 carbon atoms
    (C) 1 carbon atom
    (D) 19 carbon atoms
    Answer: A
    Explanation: Arachidonic acid has 20 carbon atoms including the carboxyl carbon. Molecular formula - C_20H_32O_2.

Q4. Which of the following are aromatic amino acids?
    (A) Lysine, valine
    (B) Alanine, glycine
    (C) Phenylalanine, tryptophan   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Serine, glutamic acid
    Answer: C
    Explanation: Based on the number of amino and carboxyl groups, there are acidic (e.g., glutamic acid), basic (lysine) and neutral (valine) amino acids. Similarly, there are aromatic amino acids (tyrosine, phenylalanine, tryptophan).

Q5. Which one is the neutral amino acid?
    (A) Tryptophan
    (B) Valine   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Lysine
    (D) Tyrosine
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Sidechains that have pure hydrocarbon alkyl groups (alkane branches) or aromatic (benzene rings) are non-polar. Examples include valine, alanine, leucine, isoleucine, phenylalanine.

Q6. Lipids that are found in the cell membrane are:
    (A) Diglycerides
    (B) Phospholipids   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Triglycerides
    (D) Simple lipids
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Some lipids have phosphorous and a phosphorylated organic compound in them. These are phospholipids. They are found in the cell membrane.

Q7. Chitin occurs in the cell wall of _______.
    (A) Yeast
    (B) Bacteria
    (C) Algae
    (D) Fungi   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: The cell wall of fungi is made of chitin, complex sugar molecules. The cell wall of plants is made up of cellulose.

Q8. Which of the following is not obtained on hydrolysis of nucleic acid?
    (A) Phosphoric acid   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Purine
    (C) Pyrimidine
    (D) Pentose sugar
    Answer: A
    Explanation: Hydrolysis of nucleic acid (DNA and RNA) produces pentose sugar (ribose or deoxyribose) purine and pyrimidine. Phosphoric acid is not released on hydrolysis of DNA or RNA.

Q9. No cell could live without ______.
    (A) Chloroplast
    (B) Protein
    (C) Phytochrome
    (D) Enzyme   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: Enzymes are bio-catalyst. All the metabolic biochemical reactions are carried out with the help of the enzyme. No cell could survive without enzyme.

Q10. The most abundant organic molecule present on earth is______.
    (A) Steroids
    (B) Lipid
    (C) Protein
    (D) Cellulose   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: Cellulose is the most abundant organic molecule present on earth. Cellulose forms the cell wall of all plant cells. The cell wall is the outermost protective layer of the cell.

Q11. 98% of a living body is formed of six elements- C, H, N, O and ________.
    (A) Ca and P
    (B) S and Mg
    (C) Mg and Na
    (D) P and S   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: Everything in the universe is made up of elements. The body of living organisms contains mainly six elements. These are carbon, hydrogen, oxygen phosphorous, and sulphur. In fact, 98% of the living body is formed on these six elements.

Q12. Element located in the centre of the porphyrin ring of chlorophyll is ______.
    (A) Mg   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Ca
    (C) Mn
    (D) K

    Explanation: Chlorophyll molecule consists of a porphyrin ring and a long tail like a chain of carbon and hydrogen. The centre of the porphyrin ring is occupied by Mg ions.

Q13. What should be TDS of Ca^++ and Mg^++ in soft water?

    (C) Up to 200 ppm

Q14. Identify the incorrect pair in the following:

Q15. Most abundant mineral in body fluids are:
    (A) Mg^2+ and I^-
