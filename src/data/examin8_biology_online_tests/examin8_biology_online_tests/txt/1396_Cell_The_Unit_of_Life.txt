Q1. New cells generate from:
    (A) Embryo
    (B) Abiotic materials
    (C) Regeneration of old cells
    (D) Pre-existing cells   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: All living organisms are composed of cells and products of cells.all cells arise from pre-existing cells.

Q2. Who first saw and described a live cell?
    (A) R. Virchow
    (B) T. Schwann
    (C) A.V. Leeuwenhoek   <-- CORRECT
    (D) M. Schleiden
    Answer: C
    Explanation: The first man to witness a live cell under a microscope was Anton van Leeuwenhoek, who in 1674 described the algae Spirogyra. Van Leeuwenhoek probably also saw bacteria.

Q3. Cytoskeleton is made of.
    (A) Calcium carbonate granules
    (B) Cellulose microfibrils
    (C) Protienaceous filaments   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Cellulose deposits
    Answer: C
    Explanation: Cytoskeleton is a series of intercellular proteins that help a cell with shape, support andmovement. So, cytoskeleton is made up of proteinaceous filaments.

Q4. Select the incorrect pairing:
    (A) Thylakoid - chloroplast
    (B) Digestive enzyme - Lysosome
    (C) Cristae - Mitochondria
    (D) Chromosome - RNA   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: Mitochondria contain cristae, finger-like projection of inner layer, thylakoid is present in chloroplast and lysosome is a digestive enzyme but RNA is absent in Chromosome.

Q5. Which is common in plant and animal cells?
    (A) Plastids
    (B) Centrioles
    (C) Mitochondria   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Central vacuoles
    Answer: C
    Explanation: Mitochondria are present in both plants as well as animal cells. Plastids and central vacuoles are present in plant cells and centrioles are present in only animal cells.

Q6. Animal cell does not possess:
    (A) Plasmodesmata   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Ribosomes
    (C) Centriole
    (D) Plasmalemma
    Answer: A
    Explanation: An animal cell does not possess plasmodesmata but they contain ribosomes, centriole, and plasmalemma. Plasmodesmata are the connection between two plant cells.

Q7. The ability of a living somatic nucleated cell to form the complete organism is called:
    (A) Cellular totipotancy   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Regeneration
    (C) Morphogenesis
    (D) Cell differentiation
    Answer: A
    Explanation: Cellular Totipotency is the ability of a single cell to produce all cell types and to organize them into an entire organism when cultured in a suitable culture medium at the appropriate temperature and aeration conditions. Spores and Zygote are examples of totipotent cells.

Q8. The most recent and accepted model of the plasma membrane is:
    (A) Lamellar model
    (B) Fluid-Mosaic model   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Danielli and Davson model
    (D) Robertson model
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Fluid-Mosaic model is the most recent and accepted model of the plasma membrane. This model was proposed by Singer and Nicolson in 1972. The quasi-fluid nature of lipid enables the lateral movement of proteins within the overall bilayer.

Q9. Each centriole is surrounded by dense amorphous protoplasmic masses called:
    (A) Cytoplasmic particles
    (B) Massules   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Cell granules
    (D) Meta-molecule
    Answer: B
    Explanation: All animal cell has two small organelles called centrioles. Each centriole is surrounded by dense amorphous protoplasmic masses called Massules.

Q10. The photograph or diagram of metaphase chromosome arranged in homologous pair according to their length and thickness, the position of the centromere is called ________.
    (A) Idiogram   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Photogram
    (C) MRi
    (D) Chromogram
    Answer: A
    Explanation: Idiogram is a diagrammatic or systematized representation of the chromosome complement of an individual, species, or another group of organisms, indicating the number of chromosomes and their lengths, arm length ratio, centromere position, banding pattern.

Q11. The membrane complex formed in the folding of the plasma membrane in prokaryotic cells is called ________.
    (A) Vacuoles
    (B) Mesosome   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Mitochondria
    (D) Centrioles
    Answer: B
    Explanation: A special membranous structure is a mesosome which is formed by the extensions of the plasma membrane into the cell.

Q12. The smallest free-living organism is______.
    (A) Bacteria
    (B) Virus
    (C) Viroids
    (D) PPLOs   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: PPLO (Pleuro Pneumonia Like Organisms). They are generally smaller and multiply more rapidly than the eukaryotic cells.

Q13. In certain bacteria, the glycocalyx is formed into loose sheath layer is called as ________.
    (A) Cell membrane
    (B) Cell wall
    (C) Slime layer   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Capsule
    Answer: C
    Explanation: Glycocalyx differs in composition and thickness among different bacteria. It could be a loose sheath called the slime layer in some, while in others it may be thick and tough called the capsule. The cell wall determines the shape of the cell and provides strong structural support.

Q14. The bacterial flagellum consists of
    (A) Filament ,basal body, pili
    (B) Filament, hook and basal body   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Filament, hook, and fimbriae
    (D) Filament, pili, and basal body
    Answer: B
    Explanation: A bacterial flagellum has 3 basic parts: a filament, a hook, and a basal body.
The filament is the rigid, helical structure that extends from the cell surface.
The hook is a flexible coupling between the filament and the basal body.
The basal body consists of a rod and a series of rings that anchor the flagellum to the cell wall and the cytoplasmic membrane.

Q15. The rod-shaped bacteria is called as_______.
    (A) Spherical
    (B) Vibrio
    (C) Bacillus   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Spiral
    Answer: C
    Explanation: The shapes of bacteria are bacillus (rod-like), coccus (spherical), vibrio (comma-shaped) and spirillum (spiral).

    (B) Proline   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Glycolipids
    (D) Cholesterol

Q6. Lipid molecules of plasma membrane occur
    (A) In series
    (B) Scattered
    (C) Parallel   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Alternately
    Answer: C
    Explanation: Phospholipids found in the plasma membrane is a lipid bilayer having a hydrophilic region called the head and a hydrophobic region called the tail. The hydrophilic region can interact with water due to the presence of a phosphate group which is polar, like water. The membrane is in contact with water on both the inner and outer sides. The hydrophilic heads arrange automatically on the outer side of the bilayer due to polarity and hydrophobic heads are facing each other between the bilayer, thus arranging all lipid molecules parallel to each other.

Q7. The plasma membrane consists mainly of:
    (A) Proteins embedded in a carbohydrate bilayer
    (B) Proteins embedded in a phospholipids bilayer   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Phospholipids embedded in protein bilayer
    (D) Proteins embedded in a polymer of glucose molecules
    Answer: B
    Explanation: The plasma membrane is composed of lipids that are arranged in a bilayer. Also, the lipids are arranged within the membrane with the polar head towards the outer sides and the hydrophobic tails towards the inner part. The membrane proteins can be classified as integral or peripheral. Peripheral proteins lie on the surface.

    (D) Golgi bodies   <-- CORRECT

Q11. The vacuoles are bound by the single membrane is called as_________.
    (A) Cisternae

Q12. The chromosome having centromeres at one terminal end is called ________.
    (A) Submetacentric
    (B) Telocentric   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Acrocentric
    (D) Metacentric

For example, the standard house mouse karyotype has only telocentric chromosomes. Humans do not possess telocentric chromosomes.

Q13. Nucleoplasm otherwise called as ______.
    (A) Nuclear matrix   <-- CORRECT

    (C) Nuclear pores
    (D) Nucleolus
    Answer: A
    Explanation: The nuclear matrix or the nucleoplasm contains nucleolus and chromatin. The nucleoli are spherical structures present in the nucleoplasm.

    Explanation: Lysosomes are found to be very rich in almost all types of hydrolytic enzymes (hydrolases - lipases, proteases, carbohydrases) optimally active at the acidic pH. These enzymes are capable of digesting carbohydrates, proteins, lipids, and nucleic acids.

Q15. Mitochondria is called Powerhouse of the cell because:
    (A) Presence of matrix
    (B) Formation of ATP   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Presence of DNA
    (D) 70S ribosomes

    Explanation: Prokaryotic and Eukaryotic cells and differentiated on the basis of the number of chromosomes, presence or absence of nuclear membrane and presence and absence of membrane-bounded organelles. The cell wall is not a difference.

Q14. The inner membrane of mitochondria thrown into folds to form finger-like structure is called ________.
    (A) Matrix
    (B) Cristae   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Amyloplast
    (D) Inter membrane space
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Mitochondria are double membranous structure. The inner membrane of mitochondria thrown into folds to form finger-like structure is called cristae.

Q15. Who first saw and described a live cell?
    (A) R. Virchow
    (B) T. Schwann
    (C) A.V. Leeuwenhoek   <-- CORRECT
    (D) M. Schleiden
    Answer: C
    Explanation: The first man to witness a live cell under a microscope was Anton van Leeuwenhoek, who in 1674 described the algae Spirogyra. Van Leeuwenhoek probably also saw bacteria.
