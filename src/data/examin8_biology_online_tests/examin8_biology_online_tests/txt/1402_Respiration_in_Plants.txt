Q1. An organism which depends on the dead and decaying organic matter is__________.
    (A) Saprophytes   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Autotrophs
    (C) Carnivores
    (D) Herbivorous
    Answer: A
    Explanation: A saprophyte or saprotroph is an organism that gets its energy from dead and decaying organic matter. This may be decaying pieces of plants or animals. This means that saprophytes are heterotrophs. They are consumers in the food chain.

Q2. In plants, the gaseous exchange takes place in:
    (A) Stomata
    (B) Stomata and Root   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Stems
    (D) Root
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Plants, unlike animals, have no special systems for breathing or gaseous exchange. Stomata and lenticels allow gaseous exchange by diffusion.

Q3. Respiration is:
    (A) Both anabolic and catabolic
    (B) Amphibolic process
    (C) Catabolic process   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Anabolic process
    Answer: C
    Explanation: Catabolism is the process of break down any large substance into smaller ones. In respiration, glucose is broken down into carbon dioxide and water molecules. Thus it is referred to as a catabolic reaction.

Q4. Pasteur effect can be studied in case of ________.
    (A) Sccharomyces   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Nostoc
    (C) Pinnularia
    (D) Penicillium
    Answer: A
    Explanation: Reduction in consumption of respiratory substrate when the mode of respiration is changed from aerobic to anaerobic is called pasture effect. It is studied in the case of Saccharomyces.

Q5. Glycolysis occurs in:
    (A) Mitochondria
    (B) Chloroplast
    (C) Cell membrane
    (D) Cytoplasm   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: Glycolysis means the splitting of glucose. The first step of the process occurs in the cytoplasm of the cell and yields a small amount of energy (measured in units of ATP) and two molecules of pyruvate. The other steps occur in the mitochondria. It is an ongoing process as your cells have a constant need for energy.

Q6. Glycolysis is also known as:
    (A) TCA cycle
    (B) EMP pathway   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Photophosphorylation
    (D) Calvin cycle
    Answer: B
    Explanation: The scheme of glycolysis was given by Gustav Embden, Otto Meyerhof, and J. Parnas, and is often referred to as the EMP pathway.

Q7. The complete combustion of glucose produces:
    (A) Pyurvic acid
    (B) Ethanol
    (C) Lactic acid
    (D) Carbon dioxide and oxygen   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: The complete combustion of glucose, which produces carbon dioxide and water as end products, yields energy most of which is given out as heat.

Q8. Respiratory Quotient for the reaction C_57H_107O_6 + 80O_2$\rightarrow$ 57CO_2 + 52H_2O is:
    (A) 0.071
    (B) 0.71   <-- CORRECT
    (C) 1.71
    (D) 1.7
    Answer: B
    Explanation: RQ is equal to the number of carbon dioxide molecules produced divided by the number of oxygen molecules used during respiration. Here, RQ = $\frac{57}{80}$ = 0.71.

Q9. What is the final product of Glycolysis?
    (A) Pyruvic acid   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Glucose
    (C) Acetyl CoA
    (D) Malic acid
    Answer: A
    Explanation: Glycolysis involves the breaking down of sugar (generally glucose, although fructose and other sugars may be used) into more manageable compounds in order to produce energy. The net end products of glycolysis are two Pyruvate, two NADH, and two ATP.

Q10. How many net ATP are produced in Glycolysis?
    (A) 2 ATP   <-- CORRECT
    (B) 8 ATP
    (C) 36 ATP
    (D) 16 ATP
    Answer: A
    Explanation: At the end of glycolysis pathway, 2 net ATP molecules at step 6 and step 9 are produced.

Q11. The enzyme which converts glucose into glucose -6- phosphate is _______.
    (A) Invertase
    (B) Dehydrogenase
    (C) Succinyl CoA
    (D) Hexokinase   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: Hexokinase is an enzyme that phosphorylates hexoses (six-carbon sugars), forming hexose phosphate. In most organisms, glucose is the most important substrate of hexokinases, and glucose-6-phosphate is the most important product.

Q12. How many step reactions are involved in glycolysis?
    (A) 12
    (B) 8
    (C) 10   <-- CORRECT
    (D) 9
    Answer: C
    Explanation: Glycolysis is a series of reactions and extracts energy from glucose by splitting it into two three-carbon molecules called pyruvates. Glycolysis is an ancient metabolic pathway, meaning that it evolved long ago, and it is found in the great majority of organisms alive today. Glycolysis has ten steps.

Q13. The partial breakdown of glucose takes place in __________.
    (A) Fermentation   <-- CORRECT
    (B) ETS cycle
    (C) Respiration
    (D) TCA cycle
    Answer: A
    Explanation: Fermentation is an anaerobic breakdown of carbohydrates in which an organic molecule is the final electron acceptor. It does not involve an electron transport system. Furthermore, it is a partial breakdown of glucose giving a little energy, 2 net ATPs per glucose by the way of substrate-level phosphorylation.

Q14. Which one is incorrect about Glycolysis?
    (A) No carbon dioxide is evolved
    (B) It occurs inside the cytoplasm
    (C) It is a cyclic pathway   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Oxygen is not required for Glycolysis
    Answer: C
    Explanation: Glycolysis is not a cyclic pathway and carbon dioxide gas is not released during this cycle. Oxygen is not required for Glycolysis process. Glycolysis occurs in the cytoplasm of the cell.

Q15. During cellular respiration, pyruvic acid is reduced to lactic acid in the presence of:
    (A) Lactate dehydrogenase   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Pyruvate dehydrogenase
    (C) Dehydrogenase
    (D) NADH dehydrogenase

Some of the organs relatively rich in LDH are the heart, kidney, liver, and muscle.

Q1. Energy is stored in the form of _______.
    (A) FAD
    (B) ATP   <-- CORRECT
    (C) NADH
    (D) ADP
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Cells store energy in a molecule called adenosine triphosphate (abbreviated as ATP). The adenosine molecule has three phosphate groups attached to it (triphosphate means three phosphates) which are held together by high energy bonds. If one of these bonds is broken, a great amount of energy is released which can be used in an endergonic reaction.

    (A) Glucose to pyruvic acid   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Glucose to fructose
    (C) Glucose to citric acid

    Answer: A
    Explanation: Glycolysis is the metabolic pathway that converts glucose C_6H_12O_6, into pyruvate, CH_3COCOO^− + H^+. The free energy released in this process is used to form the high-energy molecules ATP (adenosine triphosphate) and NADH (reduced nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide).

Q13. Metabolism of one palmitic acid molecules yields_______.
    (A) 36 ATP
    (B) 136 ATP
    (C) 129   <-- CORRECT
    (D) 56 ATP
    Answer: C
    Explanation: Energy Yield of Palmitic Acid (16C):
8 Acetyl-CoA (1 for each round + the remaining from the last two carbons) 7 FADH_2 7 NADH.
Each Acetyl-CoA yields 12 ATPs, thus 8 Acetyl-CoA will yield 96 ATPs. Each FADH_2 yields 2 ATPs while each NADH yields 3 ATPs
8 Acetyl-CoA $\times$ 12 = 96 ATPs
7 FADH_2 $\times$ 2 = 14 ATPs
7 NADH $\times$ 3 = 21 ATPs
The total energy yield is 131 ATPs, but the activation of the fatty acid (step 1) requires 2 ATPs so the net yield of energy is 129 ATPs for a molecule of palmitic acid (16 carbon).
