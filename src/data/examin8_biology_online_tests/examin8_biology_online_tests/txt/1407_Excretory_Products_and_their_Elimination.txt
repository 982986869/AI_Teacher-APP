Q1. Blood enters glomerular capillaries through ________ arteriole & leaves through ________ arteriole.
    (A) distributing, collecting
    (B) afferent, efferent   <-- CORRECT
    (C) radial, collecting
    (D) efferent, afferent
    Answer: B
    Explanation: The blood plasma is filtered through the capillaries of the glomerulus into the capsule. The Bowman's capsule empties the filtrate into the proximal tubule that is also part of the duct system of the nephron. A glomerulus receives its blood supply from an afferent arteriole of the renal circulation.

Q2. A renal corpuscle is:
    (A) Nephron
    (B) Malpighian tubule
    (C) Malpighian body   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Bowman’s capsule
    Answer: C
    Explanation: Renal corpuscle, also called malpighian body, filtration unit of vertebrate nephrons, functional units of the kidney. It consists of a knot of capillaries (glomerulus) surrounded by a double-walled capsule (Bowman's capsule) that opens into a tubule.

Q3. Homeostasis in the body is largely maintained by:
    (A) Egestion and Osmoregulation
    (B) Excretion and Osmoregulation   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Osmoregulation, Excretion, and Egestion
    (D) Excretion and Egestion
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Homeostasis is the maintenance of constant body temperature. It is mainly maintained by excretion and osmoregulation, which is the function of the kidney.

Q4. The urinary bladder is absent in:
    (A) Kangaroos
    (B) Camels
    (C) Snakes   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Monkey
    Answer: C
    Explanation: Urinary bladder is absent in snake and bird because the urine of these is semi-solid consisting mainly of uric acid. The primary function of the urinary bladder is to store urine in mammals. The urinary bladder cannot store semi-solid urine.

Q5. Ascending limb of Henle’s loop is not permeable for:
    (A) K^+
    (B) Cl^-
    (C) Na^+
    (D) H_2O   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: Ascending limb of Henle’s is not permeable to water. The descending limb is although permeable to water. Countercurrent exchange takes place between the loop of Henle’s and arteries surrounding it.

Q6. The physiological unit of a kidney is:
    (A) Juxtaglomerular apparatus
    (B) Proximal convoluted tubule
    (C) Distal convoluted tubule
    (D) Uriniferous tubule   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: Nephrons are the physical unit or functional unit of the kidney. Millions of nephrons are present in each kidney but a physiological unit of the kidney is uriniferous tubules.

Q7. The adult human kidney is metanephric. So, first embryonic tubules formed in vertebrates during embryonic development are:
    (A) Mesonephric
    (B) Metanephric
    (C) Amphinephric
    (D) Pronephric   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: The adult human kidney is metanephric, the third and final excretory organ that develops in a vertebrate embryo. First embryonic tubules formed in vertebrates during embryonic development are pronephric.

Q8. Urinary bladder helps to:
    (A) Stores urine for reabsorbing water from it
    (B) Concentrate urea in urine
    (C) Detoxify excess urine
    (D) Void urine when desired   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: The urinary bladder is a hollow elastic organ that functions as the body's urine storage tank. Urine produced by the kidneys flows through the ureters to the urinary bladder, where is it stored before passing into the urethra and exiting the body.

Q9. The part of a nephron which adds some material to the filtrate is:
    (A) Loop of Henle
    (B) Bowman’s capsule
    (C) Proximal convoluted tubule
    (D) Distal convoluted tubule   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: Each nephron consists of Bowman’s cup, tubules, and loops of Henle’s. The filtration of blood occurs in glomerulus cells present in Bowman’s cup. Some materials are added to the initial filtrate in the distal convoluted tubule.

Q11. Vasa recta is absent or reduced in:
    (A) Bowman’s capsule
    (B) Juxtamedullary nephrons
    (C) PCT
    (D) Cortical nephrons   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: The efferent arteriole emerging from the glomerulus forms a fine capillary network around the renal tubule called the peritubular capillaries. A minute vessel of this network runs parallel to Henle’s loop forming a ‘U’ shaped vasa recta. Vasa recta are absent or highly reduced in cortical nephrons.

Q12. Towards the centre of the inner concave surface of the kidney is a notch called:
    (A) Hilum   <-- CORRECT

    Explanation: On the medial margin of the kidney is a concave region called the renal hilum. The renal hilum is the entrance to the renal sinus. Structures such as the renal veins, artery, nerves, and lymphatic vessels are located in the renal hilum.

Q4. The epithelial cells of Bowman’s capsule are called:
    (A) Podocytes   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Calyces
    (C) Filtration slits
    (D) Slit pores
    Answer: A
    Explanation: Podocytes are cells in the Bowman's capsule in the kidneys that wrap around capillaries of the glomerulus. The Bowman's capsule filters the blood, retaining large molecules such as proteins while smaller molecules such as water, salts, and sugars are filtered as the first step in the formation of urine.

Q5. Conditional reabsorption of sodium ions and water takes place in_______.
    (A) PCT
    (B) DCT   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Collecting duct
    (D) Henle’s loop
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Distal Convoluted Tubule (DCT): Conditional reabsorption of Na^+ and water takes place in this segment. DCT is also capable of reabsorption of HCO_3^- and selective secretion of hydrogen and potassium ions and NH_3 to maintain the pH and sodium-potassium balance in the blood.

Q6. The filtration fraction is the ratio of GFR to RPF where both the values are in ml/min and FF is expressed in percentage. Calculate FF for a normal adult human being, if RPF= 600ml/min:
    (A) 10.38%
    (B) 2.08%
    (C) 20.83%   <-- CORRECT
    (D) 20.73%
    Answer: C
    Explanation: Filtration fraction is the ratio of glomerular filtration rate (GFR) to the renal plasma flow (RPF). GFR of a normal human is 120 ml/min. so,
FF = ${GFR\over RPF} \times 100$
= ${120\over 600} \times 100$ = 20%.

Q10. If in glomerular capillaries, pressure of blood is 75 mm Hg, osmotic pressure of plasma proteins is 30 mm Hg, and hydrostatic pressure of fluid in renal tubule is 25 mm Hg then net force or the filtration pressure will be calculated as :
    (A) 75 – [ 30 + 20 ] mm Hg   <-- CORRECT
    (B) 75 – [ 30 – 20 ] mm Hg
    (C) 75 + [ 30+20 ] mm Hg
    (D) 75 + [ 30 – 20 ] mm Hg
    Answer: A
    Explanation: Net force of filtration pressure is equal to the blood pressure of glomerular capillaries minus sum of the osmotic pressure of plasma and hydrostatic pressure of fluid in renal tubule. So, filtration pressure = 75 – [ 30 + 20 ] mm Hg.

Q14. Which one of the following is correct with reference to haemodialysis?
    (A) Anti-heparin is added prior to haemodialysis.
    (B) It absorbs and resends excess of ions.
    (C) The dialysis unit has a coiled semi permeable cellophane tube.   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Nitrogenous wastes are removed by active transport
    Answer: C
    Explanation: In haemodialysis, blood drained from a convenient artery is pumped into a dialysing unit after adding an anticoagulant like heparin. The unit contains a coiled cellophane tube surrounded by a fluid (dialysing fluid) having the same composition as that of plasma except for the nitrogenous wastes. The porous cellophane membrane of the tube allows the passage of molecules based on concentration gradient. As nitrogenous wastes are absent in the dialysing fluid, these substances freely move out, thereby clearing the blood. The cleared blood is pumped back to the body through a vein after adding anti-heparin to it.
