Q1. Which of the following is not a mixed/composite gland?
    (A) Pancreas
    (B) Ovary
    (C) Pineal   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Testis
    Answer: C
    Explanation: A gland that contains both exocrine and endocrine portions is called a mixed or composite gland. These glands secrete both hormones and enzymes. The pineal gland is an example of an endocrine gland.

Q2. By chemical nature, hormones are never:
    (A) Steroids
    (B) Proteins
    (C) Monosaccharide derivatives   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Amino acid derivatives
    Answer: C
    Explanation: Hormones are generally protein components having amino acids as well as steroids. Hormones are generally not monosaccharide derivatives in nature.

Q3. Glucocorticoids regulate the metabolism of:
    (A) Proteins and carbohydrates
    (B) Fats and carbohydrates
    (C) Carbohydrates
    (D) Carbohydrates, lipids, and proteins   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: The name glucocorticoid (glucose + cortex + steroid) is composed of its role in the regulation of glucose metabolism, synthesis in the adrenal cortex, and its steroidal structure. The principal glucocorticoid hormone is cortisol, which regulates the metabolism of proteins, carbohydrates, and lipids.
