Q1. Monstera has:
    (A) Adventitious roots   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Tertiary root
    (C) Fibrous root system
    (D) Tap root system
    Answer: A
    Explanation: In some plants, like grass, Monstera, and the banyan tree, roots arise from parts of the plant other than the radicle and are called adventitious roots.

Q2. The root is covered at the apex by a thimble-like structure called:
    (A) Region of maturation
    (B) Region of meristematic activity
    (C) Root hair
    (D) Root cap   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: The root is covered at the apex by a thimble-like structure called the root cap.

Q3. The part that develops from the plumule of the embryo of a germinating seed is:
    (A) Flower
    (B) Fruit
    (C) Stem   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Leaf
    Answer: C
    Explanation: The stem develops from the plumule of the embryo of a germinating seed.

Q4. Rhizophora possess________.
    (A) Modified root
    (B) Prop roots
    (C) Stilt root
    (D) Pneumatophores   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: Rhizophora possess Pneumatophores

Q5. Roots developed from parts of the plant other than radicle are called
    (A) Nodular root
    (B) Adventitious roots   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Taproot
    (D) Fibrous root
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Roots developed from parts of the plant other than radicle are called adventitious roots.

Q6. The small lateral outgrowth of the leaf base which protects the young leaf and its axillary buds in the young stage is called _______.
    (A) Bracts
    (B) Stipules   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Petiolate
    (D) Pulvinus
    Answer: B
    Explanation: The leaf base bear two lateral small leaf like structures called stipules.

Q7. Potato is a modified______.
    (A) Root
    (B) Stem   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Node
    (D) Ovary
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Potato is a modified stem as it contains nodes in the form of eyes on potato tubers which germinate to produce new potato plants if placed in suitable conditions.

Q8. The type of aestivation shown by the diagram below is
    (A) Twisted
    (B) Vexillary   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Valvate
    (D) Imbricate
    Answer: B
    Explanation: The diagram shows which is vexillary aestivation.
Vexillary aestivation is a mode of aestivation in which one large upper petal folds over, and covers, the other smaller petals, as in most papilionaceous plants.

Q9. The axillary buds of stems may also get modified into woody, straight and pointed thorns in______.
    (A) Mint
    (B) Jasmine
    (C) Opuntia
    (D) Citrus   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: The axillary buds of stems may also get modified into woody, straight, and pointed thorns in Citrus and Bougainvillea. They protect plants from browsing animals.

Q10. A lateral branch with short internodes and each node bearing a rosette of leaves and a tuft of roots are found in _____.
    (A) Strawberry
    (B) Pineapple
    (C) Chrysanthemum
    (D) Pistia   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: A lateral branch with short internodes and each node bearing a rosette of leaves and a tuft of roots are found in aquatic plants such as Pistia and Eicchornia.

Q11. Which part of the plant help to hold the blade to light?
    (A) Stipule
    (B) Lamina
    (C) Petiole   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Leaf base
    Answer: C
    Explanation: The petiole help hold the blade to light. Long thin flexible petioles allow leaf blades to flutter in wind, thereby cooling the leaf and bringing fresh air to the leaf surface.

Q12. Which part of the plant store food in garlic?
    (A) Fruit
    (B) Fleshy leaves   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Stem
    (D) Root
    Answer: B
    Explanation: The fleshy leaves of onion and garlic store food.

Q13. The correct arrangement of the zones as seen in the vertical section of root is
 Root hair zone
 Zone of meristem
 Root cap zone
 Zone of maturation
 Zone of elongation
    (A) A, B, C, D, E
    (B) D, E, A, C, B
    (C) E, D, C, B, A
    (D) C, B, E, A, D   <-- CORRECT

    Explanation: The correct arrangement of the zones as seen in the vertical section of root is root cap zone, region of meristematic activity, region of elongation, region of maturation and root hair region.

Q14. Phylloclade are:
    (A) Green succulent stems of indefinite growth   <-- CORRECT
    (B) One internodes long stem
    (C) Leaf modifications
    (D) Modified petiole
    Answer: A
    Explanation: Phylloclade are green succulent stems of indefinite growth that perform the function of photosynthesis due to the presence of chlorophyll.

Q15. Select the insectivorous plant from the following:
    (A) Cuscuta
    (B) Monotropa
    (C) Santlum
    (D) Drosera   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: Those plants that eat insect to fulfil its protein requirement are called insectivorous plants. They have modified leaves to capture insects. Drosera is an insectivorous plant.

Q1. 120^o phyllotaxy occurs in _________.
    (A) Octastichous
    (B) Tristichous   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Distichous
    (D) Pentstichous
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Phyllotaxy is the arrangement of leaves on a plant stem. 120^o phyllotaxy occurs in a Tristichous arrangement in which three leaves are arranged at each node.

Q2. Parallel venation is present in _________.
    (A) Monocotyledons   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Dalbergia
    (C) polycotyledons
    (D) Dicotyledons
    Answer: A
    Explanation: Parallel venation is the characteristic features of monocots. Dicots possess reticulate venation. The arrangement of veins on the leaf is called venation.

Q3. Which is not a function of leaves?
    (A) Photosynthesis
    (B) Transpiration
    (C) Gaseous exchange
    (D) Formation of new buds   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: Leaves perform the function of photosynthesis, exchange of gases, and transpiration to lose excess of water. The formation of new buds is not a function of leaves.

Q4. In Ocimum sanctum (tulsi), stem is_____.
    (A) Cylindrical
    (B) Triangular
    (C) Round
    (D) Quadrangular   <-- CORRECT

    Explanation: The stem of Ocimum sanctum or tulsi is quadrangular in shape. The stem is covered with hairy projection.

Q5. Bladders of Utricularia and Pitchers of Nepenthes are a modification of______.
    (A) Leaves   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Roots
    (C) Stems
    (D) Flowers
    Answer: A
    Explanation: Bladders of Utricularia and Pitcher of Nepenthes are a modification of leaves. They are modified to catch insects that are insectivorous plants.

Q6. A plant in which adventitious roots develop chlorophyll and perform photosynthesis is______.
    (A) Dischidia   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Tinospora

    (A) Racemose   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Corymb
    (C) Spike
    (D) Cymose
    Answer: A
    Explanation: In racemose inflorescence indefinite growth occurs and bears a number of flowers due to the active growing point.

Q8. A scar on the seed coat through which the developing seeds were attached to the fruit is called _________.
    (A) Tegmen
    (B) Micropyle
    (C) Hilum   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Testa

Q9. Name the type of thalamus in which the ovary is at top and stamens, petals and sepals are born below.
    (A) Semi-epigyny
    (B) Epigyny
    (C) Hypogyny   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Perigyny
    Answer: C
    Explanation: In hypergamous flowers, the ovary is at the tops and stamens, petals, and sepals are borne below on the thalamus. The ovary is called superior.

Q10. The fruit develops to form a whole inflorescence is called ________.
    (A) Aggregate fruits
    (B) Composite fruits   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Complex fruit
    (D) Simple fruit
    Answer: B
    Explanation: True fruit is the fruit that develops from ovary only. In composite fruits, the whole inflorescence develops into fruits as in case of jackfruit.

Q11. The cup-like structure formed by the adnation of sepals, petals, and stamens is ______.
    (A) Hypanthodium
    (B) Cyathium
    (C) Capitulum
    (D) Hypanthium   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: In angiosperms, a hypanthium is a structure where basal portions of the calyx, the corolla, and the stamens form a cup-shaped tube.

    (A) Ornithischia
    (B) Therapsida   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Silusoidea

Q13. Bat is member of an order________.
    (A) Lagomorpha
    (B) Urodela
    (C) Chiroptera   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Hymenoptera
    Answer: C
    Explanation: Bat is a member of the order Chiroptera of class Mammalia. Bats are only mammals to have achieved powered flight in which forelimb is modified as wings.

Q14. Which one of the following has the highest number of species in nature?
    (A) Fungi
    (B) Birds
    (C) Mammals
    (D) Insects   <-- CORRECT

    Explanation: Insects have the highest number of species in nature. Insect belongs to arthropods family of class Mammalia. They are characterized by jointed legs.

Q15. One animal that does not perform locomotion is ______.
    (A) Sycon   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Euglena
    (C) Sepia
    (D) Nereis
    Answer: A
    Explanation: Sycon does not perform locomotion, although it is an animal. Sycon remains attached to the substratum and does not move from one place to another.

    (A) Argemone   <-- CORRECT

Q2. The form of the thalamus in which the ovary is at top and stamens, petals, and sepals are borne below is called ________.
    (A) Epigyny
    (B) Semi-epigyny
    (C) Hypogyny   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Perigyny
    Answer: C
    Explanation: In the hypogynous flower, the gynoecium occupies the highest position while the other parts are situated below it. The ovary in such flowers is said to be superior.

Q3. Which plant shows valvate aestivation?
    (A) China rose
    (B) Calotropis   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Gulmohur
    (D) Pea

    (A) Polyadelphous
