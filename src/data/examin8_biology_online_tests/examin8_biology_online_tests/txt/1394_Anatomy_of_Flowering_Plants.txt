Q1. The tissue present in all organs of plant is________.
    (A) Parenchyma   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Collenchyma
    (C) Chlorenchyma
    (D) Sclerenchyma
    Answer: A
    Explanation: Generally, all the organs of a plant are made up of parenchyma cells.

Q2. Which meristem contributes to the formation of the primary plant body?
    (A) Both lateral and apical meristem
    (B) Lateral meristem
    (C) Both apical and intercalary meristem   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Apical meristem
    Answer: C
    Explanation: Both apical meristems and intercalary meristems are primary meristems because they appear early in the life of a plant and contribute to the formation of the primary plant body.

Q3. Sclereids belongs to_______.
    (A) Collenchyma
    (B) Sclerenchyma   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Parenchyma
    (D) Fibres
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Sclereids belongs to sclerenchyma complex permanent tissue present in hard parts of plants. They are generally hard and founds in rough fruits and stem.

Q4. The parenchymatous cells which lie between the xylem and the phloem are called ________.
    (A) Conjunctive tissue   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Casparian strip
    (C) Endodermis
    (D) Pericycle
    Answer: A
    Explanation: Conjunctive tissue is a narrow strip of tissue (parenchyma or sclerenchyma) that lies between xylem and phloem bundles of the root.

Q5. A specialized parenchymatous cell, which is closely associated with sieve tube elements are:
    (A) Companion cells   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Phloem parenchyma
    (C) Xylem parenchyma
    (D) Phloem fibres
    Answer: A
    Explanation: The companion cells are specialised parenchymatous cells, which are closely associated with sieve tube elements. The sieve tube elements and companion cells are connected by pit fields present between their common longitudinal walls.

Q6. The meristems which occur at the tips of roots and shoots and produce primary tissues are called _________.
    (A) Apical meristem   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Intercalary meristem
    (C) Primary meristem
    (D) Lateral meristem
    Answer: A
    Explanation: Apical meristem is found at the apices, or tips of the plant, both the tip of the shoot and the root, and is a region of actively dividing cells. The definition is easy to remember when you break it down. An apex is a tip, the very end, of something.

Q7. A group of cells having a common origin and usually performing a common function is:
    (A) Tissues   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Cambium
    (C) Meristems
    (D) Protoderm
    Answer: A
    Explanation: A tissue is an ensemble of similar cells from the same origin that together carry out a specific function. Organs are then formed by the functional grouping together of multiple tissues.

Q8. Conjoint type of vascular bundles are common in:
    (A) Stems and leaves   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Leaves and roots
    (C) Stems, roots and leaves
    (D) Stems and roots
    Answer: A
    Explanation: In the conjoint type of vascular bundles, the xylem and phloem are situated at the same radius of vascular bundles. Such vascular bundles are common in stems and leaves. The conjoint vascular bundles usually have the phloem located only on the outer side of the xylem.

Q9. Which one is not a xylem cell?
    (A) Tracheids
    (B) Companion cells   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Xylem parenchyma
    (D) Vessels
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Xylem is a complex tissue, which consists of four kinds of cells. These cells are tracheid, vessels, xylem parenchyma and xylem fibres. Companion cell is part of phloem tissue.

Q10. Which is not the function of parenchymatous cells?
    (A) Secretion
    (B) Photosynthesis
    (C) Storage
    (D) Mechanical support   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: The parenchyma performs various functions like photosynthesis, storage, secretion.

Q11. Which among the following are elongated or tube-like cells with thick and lignified walls and tapering ends?
    (A) Xylem fibres
    (B) Vessels
    (C) Xylem parenchyma
    (D) Tracheids   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: Tracheids are elongated cells in the xylem of vascular plants that serve in the transport of water and mineral salts. Tracheids are one of two types of tracheary elements, vessel elements being the other.

Q12. The characteristic feature of which cells have lignified thickening at the corner?
    (A) Parenchyma
    (B) Sclerenchyma   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Chlorenchyma
    (D) Collechyma
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Sclerenchyma consists of long, narrow cells with thick and lignified cell walls having a few or numerous pits.

Q13. In monocotyledons, guard cell of stomata are The figure given below is the apical meristem in the root. Identify ‘A’ in the diagram.
    (A) Axillary bud
    (B) Procambium
    (C) Protoderm   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Cortex
    Answer: C
    Explanation: The part ‘A’ is protoderm in apical meristem of root.

Q14. Which is the function of companion cells?
    (A) Radial conduction of water.
    (B) To store food materials like resins, latex etc.
    (C) Controlling the functions of sieve tubes.   <-- CORRECT
    (D) To maintain the pressure gradient in the sieve tubes.
    Answer: C
    Explanation: Controlling the functions of sieve tubes.

Q15. Tracheids differ from vessels in having:
    (A) Rounded structure
    (B) Pits in walls
    (C) Pointed tips   <-- CORRECT
    (D) All of these
    Answer: C
    Explanation: Tracheids and vessels are the main water and mineral conducting tissues of xylem. Tracheid has pointed tips but vessels have blunt ends.

Q1. Epidermal appendages, unicellular or multicellular outgrowth strictly epidermal in origin is_________.
    (A) Prickles
    (B) Root hairs
    (C) Trichomes   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Scales
    Answer: C
    Explanation: Trichomes are epidermal appendages, unicellular or multicellular outgrowth strictly epidermal in origin present on the stem of plants.

Q2. In angiosperms, vascular tissue develops from:
    (A) Dematogen
    (B) Phelogen
    (C) Periblem
    (D) Plerome   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: In angiosperms, vascular tissue develops from plerome. Plerome forms the central core of the primary meristem of a plant or plant part.

Q3. Which epidermis of the leaf contains more stomata?
    (A) Both adaxial and abaxial contain same
    (B) Lateral
    (C) Adaxial
    (D) Abaxial   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: Stomata is present more on the lower surface of the leaf. The lower surface of the leaf is called abaxial part of the leaf that is away from direct sunlight to prevent an excess of water loss.

Q4. Jute fibres deteriorate because they have _______.
    (A) Low lignin
    (B) High cellulose
    (C) Low cellulose
    (D) High lignin   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: Jute fibres are made of a high amount of lignin that deteriorates in the presence of high moisture and water.

Q5. In monocotyledons, guard cell of stomata are:
    (A) Bi-concave shaped
    (B) Biconvex shaped
    (C) Dumb-bell shaped   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Bean-shaped
    Answer: C
    Explanation: In monocotyledons, the guard cell of stomata is dumb-bell shaped, thicker on top and bottom and thinner in the middle.

Q6. Which one functions as a Biological check post?
    (A) Cuticle
    (B) Pericycle
    (C) Epidermis
    (D) Endodermis   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: Endodermis forms the outermost layer of vascular bundles. Anything entering inside the plant body must cross the endodermis to be transported via xylem and phloem. So, endodermis is called a biological check post of the cell.

Q7. Which part of epidermis of the leaf contains fewer stomata?
    (A) Both adaxial and abaxial contain same
    (B) Lateral
    (C) Adaxial   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Abaxial
    Answer: C
    Explanation: There are a number of differences between abaxial and adaxial stomata. First, stomatal density is usually higher on the abaxial surface than it is on the adaxial surface of leaves.

Q8. The Casparian thickening occurs in the cells of:
    (A) Pericycle of root
    (B) Endodermis of root   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Endodermis of stem
    (D) Pericycle of stem
    Answer: B
    Explanation: The tangential, as well as radial walls of the endodermal cells, have a deposition of water-impermeable, waxy material-suberin-in the form of Casparian strips.

Q9. The vascular bundles are closed when they:
    (A) Have cambium
    (B) Lack pericycle
    (C) Lack cambium   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Have pericycle
    Answer: C
    Explanation: In the monocotyledons, the vascular bundles have no cambium present in them. Hence, since they do not form secondary tissues they are referred to as closed.

Q10. In leaves, the ground tissue consists of thin-walled chloroplast containing cells are called _________.
    (A) Parenchyma
    (B) Chlorenchyma
    (C) Mesophyll   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Sclerenchyma
    Answer: C
    Explanation: The tissues of a leaf that are located in between the layers of the epidermis and carry on photosynthesis, consisting of the palisade layer and the spongy parenchyma. Most mesophyll cells contain chloroplasts.

Q11. Which of the following do not undergo any secondary growth?
    (A) Dicotyledonous root
    (B) Dicotyledonous stem
    (C) Monocotyledonous stem
    (D) Monocotyledonous root   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: Secondary growth in monocotyledonous roots is not observed as cambium is absent between xylem and phloem in a vascular bundle.

Q12. The shape of guard cells in grasses is:
    (A) Dumb-bell   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Elliptical
    (C) Round
    (D) Kidney
    Answer: A
    Explanation: Dumb-bell-shaped guard cells are found in grasses. Dumb-bell shaped guard cells are more advanced in evolutionary terms and more efﬁcient physiologically because guard cells of grasses require fewer solutes and less water to achieve a given unit increase in the aperture​.

Q13. Monocotyledonous leaves are_________.
    (A) Dorsiventral
    (B) Round
    (C) Pinnate
    (D) Isobilateral   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: Monocotyledonous leaves are isobilateral as both the lower and upper sides of the leaves are the same.

Q14. The main seat of photosynthesis in the leaf is:
    (A) Abaxial epidermis
    (B) Spongy parenchyma
    (C) Cuticle
    (D) Palisade parenchyma   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: The palisade parenchymatous cell in the mesophyll region has numerous chloroplasts. Hence, it is the main seat of photosynthesis in leaf.

Q1. Reticulate venation is characteristic in:

Q2. Select the correct statement.
 Monocot roots do not undergo secondary growth.
 The hypodermis is a sclerenchymatous cell in dicot stems.
 Monocot roots undergo secondary growth.
 The hypodermis is collenchymatous cell in monocot stems.
    (A) (C)
    (B) (B)
    (C) (D)
    (D) (A)   <-- CORRECT

    (A) Dorsiventral leaves of monocot
    (B) Isobilateral leaves of monocot   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Dorsiventral leaves of dicot
    (D) Unifacial leaves of monocot
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Bulliform cells or motor cells are large, bubble-shaped epidermal cells that occur in groups on the upper surface of the leaves of many monocots on the upper surface of the leaf.

Q4. In arborescent monocots stem, a secondary cambium grows in the following type of vascular bundle is seen in
    (A) Monocot stem
    (B) Monocot root   <-- CORRECT

Q5. Anatomy of stem differs from the root in:

    (B) Stem hairs, absence of chloroplast an broad cortex

Q6. The stem of grasses and related plants elongate by the activity of:

    (A) Bicollateral
    (B) Radial   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Amphivessal
    (D) Collateral

Q8. The quiescent centre is found in the plant at:
    (A) Cambium
    (B) Shoot tips
    (C) Leaf tip
    (D) Root tips   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: A quiescent centre is a small group of cells with low mitotic activity. It is found at the tips of growing roots in the root meristem.

Q9. Ground tissue consists of:
    (A) All tissues except epidermis and vascular tissue   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Epidermis and cortex
    (C) All tissues internal to endodermis
    (D) All tissues external to endodermis
    Answer: A
    Explanation: The ground tissue of plants includes all tissues that are neither dermal nor vascular. It can be divided into three classes based on the nature of the cell walls.

Q10. Lacunate collenchyma occurs in stem of______.
    (A) Cucurbita   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Sunflower
    (C) Leucas
    (D) Sambucus
    Answer: A
    Explanation: Lacunate collenchyma is very much alike to angular collenchyma but the cells are regularly arranged. Thickening occurs at the inner and outer tangential wall. It is found in Cucurbita stem.

Q11. In Barley stem, vascular bundles are:

    (D) Closed and radial

Q12. Guard cells of stomata are thicker:

    (B) Outer side
    (C) Both side
    (D) Inner side   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: The cells surrounding the stomata are called guard cells. The guard cells of the inner side are thicker and that of outer side is comparatively thinner.

Q13. The function of the guard cell in stomata is to:

    (B) Transpiration
    (C) Open and close stomatal pore   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Exchange of gases

    Explanation: Opening and closing of stomatal pore of stomata is the function of guard cells. Change in osmotic potential cause the opening and closing of stomata.

Q14. Upper surface of leaf is:

    (C) Mesophyll cell

    Explanation: Epidermal cells are present on both sides of the leaves. The lower side contains more stomata. The upper surface of the leaf is called the adaxial epidermis.

Q15. Tyloses are balloon-like ingrowths in vessels developing from:

    (D) Parenchyma through the general surface of the vessel wall

    Explanation: Tyloses are balloon-like ingrowths in vessels developing from parenchyma cells through the pits of the vessel wall of the xylem of secondary heartwood.

Q1. Vascular bundles in which cambium is present between xylem and phloem is called as:
    (A) Amphivesal
    (B) Open   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Collateral
    (D) Closed
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Vascular bundles in which cambium is present between xylem and phloem are called open vascular bundles and those in which cambium is absent are called closed vascular bundles.

Q2. In angiosperms, the main water and mineral transporting elements are:
    (A) Tracheids and Vessels   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Xylem parenchyma
    (C) Xylem fibres
    (D) Primary xylem
    Answer: A
    Explanation: Tracheids and Vessels

Q3. Exarch xylem is present in______.
    (A) Both stem and roots
    (B) Roots   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Stem
    (D) Leaves
    Answer: B
    Explanation: The xylem in which protoxylem is outside and metaxylem is inside is called exarch xylem. This kind of xylem is found in the roots of dicots.

Q4. In arborescent monocots stem, a secondary cambium grows in:
    (A) Epidermal region
    (B) Hypodermal region   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Vascular region
    (D) Cuticular region
    Answer: B
    Explanation: In arborescent monocots stem, secondary cambium grows in the hypodermal region for secondary growth that increases the girth of the stem.

Q5. Which type of vascular bundle is shown in the figure below
    (A) Concentric
    (B) Amphicribal
    (C) Open
    (D) Closed   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: The vascular bundle shown below is closed and conjoint as cambium is absent between xylem and phloem tissue.

Q6. In dicot stems, the cells of cambium present between the primary xylem and primary phloem are:
    (A) Vascular cambium
    (B) Medullary cells
    (C) Intrafascicular cambium   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Interfascicular cambium
    Answer: C
    Explanation: Intrafascicular Cambium is primary meristem. It develops from the procambium of the stem apex. It is located inside the open vascular bundles, between phloem and xylem patches.

Q7. The secondary growth of woody angiosperm stem occurs by:
    (A) Cork cambium and lateral meristem
    (B) Vascular cambium and apical meristem
    (C) Vascular cambium and cork cambium   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Cork cambium and apical meristem
    Answer: C
    Explanation: The tissues involved in secondary growth are the two lateral meristems namely, vascular cambium and cork cambium.

Q8. The wood is actually a:
    (A) Secondary xylem   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Primary xylem
    (C) Secondary phloem
    (D) Primary phloem
    Answer: A
    Explanation: Wood is a secondary xylem. It is secondary growth by cambium and constitutes the bulk of the plant body in dicot stems and dicot roots.

Q9. Interfascicular cambium arise from the cells of:
    (A) Protoxylem
    (B) Phellogen
    (C) Epidermal cells
    (D) Medullary rays   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: During secondary growth, cells of medullary rays, in a line with intrafascicular cambium, become meristematic and form interfascicular cambium.
Therefore, the intrafascicular and interfascicular cambia form a ring which separates the primary xylem and primary phloem and is known as cambium ring.

Q10. The region consisting of dead elements with highly lignified walls is called ________.
    (A) Heartwood   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Autumn wood
    (C) Sapwood
    (D) Springwood
    Answer: A
    Explanation: In old trees, the greater part of secondary xylem is dark brown due to deposition of organic compounds like tannins, resins, oils, gums, aromatic substances and essential oils in the central or innermost layers of the stem. These substances make it hard, durable and resistant to the attacks of microorganisms and insects. This region comprises dead elements with highly lignified walls and is called heartwood.

Q11. Cork cambium is also known as______.
    (A) Phelloderm
    (B) Complementary cells
    (C) Accessory cells
    (D) Phellogen   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: As the stem continues to increase in girth due to the activity of vascular cambium, the outer cortical and epidermis layers get broken and need to be replaced to provide new protective cell layers. Hence, another meristematic tissue called cork cambium or phellogen develops in the cortex region.

Q12. The cells of cambial ring cut off towards pith and mature into:
    (A) Primary xylem
    (B) Secondary xylem   <-- CORRECT
    (C) Secondary phloem
    (D) Secondary medullary rays
    Answer: B
    Explanation: The cambial ring becomes active and begins to cut off new cells, both towards the inner and the outer sides. The cells cut off towards pith, mature into secondary xylem.

Q13. The phelloderm is also called _______.
    (A) Cork cambium
    (B) Cork
    (C) Periderm
    (D) Secondary cortex   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: The outer cells differentiate into cork or phellem while the inner cells differentiate into secondary cortex or phelloderm.

Q14. Aerating pores in the bark of plants is known as _______.
    (A) Lenticels   <-- CORRECT
    (B) Air pore
    (C) Respiration pores
    (D) Stomata
    Answer: A
    Explanation: Lenticels permit the exchange of gases between the outer atmosphere and the internal tissue of the stem. These occur in most woody trees.

Q15. Sclereids belongs to_______.
    (A) Fibres
    (B) Collenchyma
    (C) Parenchyma
    (D) Sclerenchyma   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: Sclereids belongs to sclerenchyma complex permanent tissue present in hard parts of plants. They are generally hard and founds in rough fruits and stem.
