Q3. The specific gravity of a material is:
    (A) the ratio of its mass to the mass of water
    (B) the ratio of its volume to the density of water
    (C) the ratio of its density to the density of water   <-- CORRECT
    (D) the ratio of its mass to the density of water
    Answer: C
    Explanation: The specific gravity of an object is the ratio between the density of an object to a reference liquid. Usually, this reference liquid is water, which has a density of 1 g/mL or 1 g/cm^3.
Water has a specific gravity equal to 1. Materials with a specific gravity less than 1 are less dense than water, and will float on the pure liquid; substances with a specific gravity more than 1 are denser than water and will sink.

    (C) transmitted and decreased
    (D) transmitted and increased
    Answer: B

$\Delta P$ is the hydrostatic pressure (given in pascals in the SI system), or the difference in pressure at two points within a fluid column, due to the weight of the fluid.

Volume flow in over A_1 = A_1V_1$\Delta$t
Volume flow out over A_2 = A_2V_2$\Delta$t
Therefore
mass in over A = $\rho$A_1V_1$\Delta$t
mass out over A = $\rho$A_2V_2$\Delta$t
So:$\rho$ A_1V_1 = $\rho$A_2V_2$\Delta$t
As volume is the same so this equation can be written as|
A_1V_1 = A_2V_2
This equation is called the continuity equation for steady one-dimensional flow.

Q7. The molecules on the surface, are:
    (A) The balanced attractions cause the surface to resist being stretched
    (B) The unbalanced attractions cause the surface to stretch less
    (C) The unbalanced attractions cause the surface to stretch more
    (D) The unbalanced attractions cause the surface to resist being stretched   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: The surface tension of liquid results from an imbalance of intermolecular attractive forces, the cohesive forces between molecules: A molecule in the bulk liquid experiences cohesive forces with other molecules in all directions. A molecule at the surface of a liquid experiences only net inward cohesive forces. The unbalanced attraction of molecules at the surface of a liquid tends to pull the molecules back into the bulk liquid leaving the minimum number of molecules on the surface. It required energy to increase the surface area of a liquid because a larger surface area contains more molecules in the unbalanced situation and this may result as the surface being stretched.

Q8. Water drops are spherical because of:
    (A) surface tension   <-- CORRECT
    (B) viscosity
    (C) elasticity
    (D) low shear strain
    Answer: A
    Explanation: In fluids, there are primarily 2 forces cohesive force and adhesive force. Cohesive force is the one that acts between the same molecules and adhesive force is the one that acts between different molecules. 
The cohesive force gives rise to a phenomenon called surface tension. In this phenomenon a fluid surface tends to pull itself inwards, causing a reduction of the exposed surface.
For a water droplet that is left alone, cohesive forces undoubtedly dominate, leading to surface tension. Surface tension makes all the molecules of water to stick with each other closely, this results in its spherical shape.

Q10. Capillary Rise of water is explained by:
    (A) low shear strain
    (B) elasticity
    (C) surface tension   <-- CORRECT
    (D) viscosity
    Answer: C
    Explanation: Capillary action(sometimes capillarity or wicking) is the ability of a liquid to flow in narrow spaces without the assistance of, or even in opposition to, external forces like gravity. The effect can be seen in the drawing up of liquids between the hairs of a paint-brush, in porous materials such as paper and plaster, in some non-porous materials such as sand and liquefied carbon fiber, or in a cell. It occurs because of intermolecular forces between the liquid and surrounding solid surfaces. If the diameter of the tube is sufficiently small, then the combination of surface tension (which is caused by cohesion within the liquid) and adhesive forces between the liquid and container wall act to propel the liquid.

Q11. An iron needle floats on the surface of the water. This phenomenon is attributed to:
    (A) Viscosity
    (B) Up thrust of liquid
    (C) Surface tension   <-- CORRECT
    (D) Gravitational forces
    Answer: C
    Explanation: The answer lies in the surface tension of the water, As the cohesive forces between the molecules are responsible for the surface tension. When the molecules on the glass or container do not have a surface to stick to, they cohere more strongly to the molecules they are associated with. Since the molecules on top have no neighbours on the top to cohere to, they associate with their other neighbours more strongly. This creates a strong bond between the top layer of molecules. The molecules inward face no net force because they all cancel out each other. However, the surface molecules face a net inward force because there is no force for them on the surface to cancel it. This causes the molecules on the surface to 'contract' and resists being broken or stretched.
When a small body like a needle is placed onto the water carefully, the surface tension will not break due to the small mass of the object. 
As long as the surface tension is undisturbed, the needle will continue to float. Agitating the water will cause the surface tension to break and hence the needle will then sink
.
