Q1. The scalar product of two vectors A and B is
    (A) vector
    (B) a scalar   <-- CORRECT
    (C) a complex number
    (D) a tensor
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Scalar product means dot product and the dot product of 2 vectors gives a scalar. For example dot product of force and displacement gives work which is scalar.

Q2. The scalar product of two vectors A and B in terms of the magnitudes and angle $\theta $ is
    (A) $\left| {\bf{A}} \right|\left| {\bf{B}} \right|{\rm{cot}}\theta $
    (B) $\left| {\bf{A}} \right|\left| {\bf{B}} \right|{\rm{cos}}\theta $   <-- CORRECT
    (C) $\left| {\bf{A}} \right|\left| {\bf{B}} \right|{\rm{tan}}\theta $
    (D) $\left| {\bf{A}} \right|\left| {\bf{B}} \right|{\rm{sin}}\theta $
    Answer: B
    Explanation: The scalar product of two vectors can be constructed by taking the component of one vector in the direction of the other and multiplying it times the magnitude of the other vector. This can be expressed in the form $\vec A.\vec B = |A||B|\cos \theta $

Q3. The scalar product of two vectors $\overrightarrow A$ and $\overrightarrow B$ in terms of the projections of the vectors on the x, y and z axis is
    (A) $\overrightarrow A$.$\overrightarrow B$ = $A_x B_x-A_y B_y-A_z B_z$
    (B) $\overrightarrow A$.$\overrightarrow B$ = $A_x B_x+A_y B_y-A_z B_z$
    (C) $\overrightarrow A$.$\overrightarrow B$ = $A_x B_x-A_y B_y+A_z B_z$
    (D) $\overrightarrow A$.$\overrightarrow B$ = $A_x B_x+A_y B_y+A_z B_z$   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: $\overrightarrow A$ = A_x i + A_y j+ A_zk
$\overrightarrow B$ = B_x i + B_y j+ B_zk
$\overrightarrow A$.$\overrightarrow B$ = (A_x i + A_y j+ A_zk). (B_x i+ B_y j+ B_zk) 
$\overrightarrow A$.$\overrightarrow B$ = A_xB_x + A_yB_y + A_zB_z , this is required result.

Q4. The scalar product of vectors is
    (A) non commutative and not distributive
    (B) commutative and not distributive
    (C) non commutative and distributive
    (D) commutative and distributive   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: The scalar product is:
 Commutative:
 $\vec A.\vec B = \vec B.\vec A$ 
 |A||B| cos $\theta$ = |B||A| cos $\theta$
 Distributive 
 $\vec A.(\vec B + \vec C) = \vec A.\vec B + \vec A.\vec C$

Q12. Physically, the notion of potential energy is applicable only to:

Q6. In an elastic collision the total kinetic energy of the system is
    (A) the same after the collision as before.   <-- CORRECT
    (B) greater than the total kinetic energy before.
    (C) is less than the total kinetic energy before.
    (D) zero after the collision.
    Answer: A
    Explanation: Kinetic energy is transferred to other forms of energy—such as thermal energy, potential energy, and sound—during the collision process. After the collision, if recovery of kinetic energy is 100% then it is called an elastic collision. So that in an elastic collision the total kinetic energy of the system is the same after the collision as before.

Q7. In an inelastic collision, the total kinetic energy of the system is
    (A) less than before the collision.   <-- CORRECT
    (B) greater than the total kinetic energy before.
    (C) same after the collision as before.
    (D) zero after the collision.
    Answer: A
    Explanation: Kinetic energy is transferred to other forms of energy—such as thermal energy, potential energy, and sound—during the collision process. After collision if recovery of kinetic energy is less than 100% then it is called inelastic collision i.e., some part of kinetic energy is not recovered. So that in an inelastic collision the total kinetic energy after the collision is less than before the collision.

Q12. On an essentially frictionless, horizontal ice rink, a skater moving at 3.0 m/s encounters a rough patch that reduces her speed to 1.65 m/s due to a friction force that is 25$\% $ of her weight. Use the work-energy theorem to find the length of this rough patch?
    (A) 1.1 m
    (B) 1.3 m   <-- CORRECT
    (C) 0.9 m
    (D) 1.5 m
    Answer: B
    Explanation: Using the work-energy theorem, change in kinetic energy = work done by the friction force

$\frac{1}{2} m\left(v_{f}^{2}-v_{i}^{2}\right)$ = -fd
$\frac{1}{2} m\left(v_{f}^{2}-v_{i}^{2}\right)$ = $-m g \times \frac{25}{100} \times d$

d = $\frac{6.2775 \times 100}{2 \times 9.8 \times 25}$ = 1.3 m
