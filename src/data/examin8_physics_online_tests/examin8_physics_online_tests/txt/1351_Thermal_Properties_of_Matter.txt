    (C) it contracts on cooling from 4^oC to 0^oC
    (D) it expands on cooling from 10^oC to 4^oC
    Answer: A
    Explanation: Liquids expand on heating and contract on cooling at moderate temperatures. But water exhibits exceptional behaviour. This behavior of water between 0^oC and 4^oC is called the anomalous expansion of water.

Q1. For an ideal gas the coefficient of volume expansion at constant pressure is:
    (A) $\frac{1}{2T}$; T is the temperature
    (B) $\frac{1}{3T}$; T is the temperature
    (C) $\frac{2}{T}$; T is the temperature
    (D) $\frac{1}{T}$; T is the temperature   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: $\gamma=\frac{\Delta V}{V \Delta T}$
PV = nRT
V = $\frac{n R T}{P}$
$P \Delta V=n R \Delta T$
$\Delta V=\frac{n R \Delta T}{P}$
$\gamma=\frac{\frac{n R \Delta T}{P}}{\frac{n R T}{P}} \times \frac{1}{\Delta T}$ = $\frac{n R \Delta T}{P} \times \frac{P}{n R T} \times \frac{1}{\Delta T}=\frac{1}{T}$

Q2. The coefficient of volume expansion is n times coefficient of linear expansion where n equals:
    (A) 3   <-- CORRECT
    (B) 4
    (C) 5
    (D) 2
    Answer: A
    Explanation: $\alpha = \left( {{{\Delta l} \over l}} \right){1 \over {\Delta T}}$ and $\gamma = \left( {{{\Delta V} \over V}} \right){1 \over {\Delta T}}$ 
Volume V = L^3
expanded length = $L(1 + \alpha \Delta T)$ 
expanded Volume = ${\left[ {L(1 + \alpha \Delta T)} \right]^3} = {L^3}(1 + {\alpha ^2}\Delta {T^2} + 2\alpha \Delta T)(1 + \alpha \Delta T)$ 
very small term is neglected
expanded volume = $V(1 + 3\alpha \Delta T)$ 
$\frac{\Delta l}{l}=a \Delta T$
$\gamma = \left( {{{\Delta V} \over V}} \right){1 \over {\Delta T}} = {{3\alpha V\Delta T} \over V}{1 \over {\Delta T}}$ 
$ \gamma = 3\alpha$

Q3. The coefficient of area expansion is n times of coefficient of linear expansion. where n equals
    (A) 3
    (B) 2   <-- CORRECT
    (C) 5
    (D) 4
    Answer: B
    Explanation: $\alpha = \left( {{{\Delta l} \over l}} \right){1 \over {\Delta T}}$ and $\beta = \left( {{{\Delta A} \over A}} \right){1 \over {\Delta T}}$ 
area A = L^2
expanded length = $L(1 + \alpha \Delta T)$ 
expanded area = ${\left[ {L(1 + \alpha \Delta T)} \right]^2} $ = $ {L^2}(1 + {\alpha ^2}\Delta {T^2} + 2\alpha \Delta T)$ 
${\alpha ^2}\Delta {T^2}$ is very small
expanded area = $A(1 + 2\alpha \Delta T)$ 
$\Delta A = 2\alpha A\Delta T$
$\beta=\left(\frac{\Delta A}{A}\right) \frac{1}{\Delta T}=\frac{2 \alpha A \Delta T}{A} \frac{1}{\Delta T}$
$\beta=2 \alpha$ 
So that n = 2

    Explanation: The melting point of lead is given by 327.3^o C.
Assume the final temperature of the system is T. Then the amount of energy released by the lead as it solidifies is given by 
ΔQ = m_leadL_lead = 0.09 kg*(2.45*10^4 J/kg) = 2205 J,
and the amount of energy released as it cools is given by :-
ΔQ = m_leadc_leadΔT = 0.09 kg*(128 J/(kg^oC))(327.3 ^oC - T) = (11.52 J/^oC)(327.3 ^oC - T).
This energy is absorbed by the iron. Thus,
2205 J + (11.52 J/^oC) (327.3^oC - T) = m_ironc_ironΔT = 0.3 kg*(448 J/(kg^oC))(T - 20 ^oC).
5975.5 J - (11.52 J/^oC)T = (134 J/^oC)T - 2688 J.
8663.5 J = (145.52 J/^oC)T.
T = 59.5^o C.

Q14. A surveyor uses a steel measuring tape that is exactly 50.000 m long at a temperature of 20$^\circ $C. The markings on the tape are calibrated for this temperature.
 What is the length of the tape when the temperature is 35^oC?
 When it is 35^oC, the surveyor uses the tape to measure a distance. The value that she reads off the tape is 35.794 m. What is the actual distance? $\alpha$ = 1.2 $\times$ 10^-5oC
    (A) 50.010 m, 35.900 m
    (B) 50.008 m, 35.600 m
    (C) 50.007 m, 35.750 m
    (D) 50.009 m, 35.800 m   <-- CORRECT
    Answer: D
    Explanation: $\alpha = {{\Delta l} \over l}{1 \over {\Delta T}}$
$\Delta l = l\alpha \Delta T$ = 50 $\times$ 1.2 $\times$ 10^-5 $\times$ 15 = 0.009m
final length = 50 + 0.009m = 50.009m
if actual distance = x
then $\Delta x = x\alpha \Delta T$
x−35.794 = x $\times$ 1.2 $\times$ 10^−5 $\times$ 15
x = 35.800m

Q15. A 200- ${\rm{c}}{{\rm{m}}^{\rm{3}}}$ glass flask is filled to the brim with mercury at 20^oC. How much mercury overflows when the temperature of the system is raised to 100$^\circ $ C.The coefficient of linear expansion of the glass is 0.40 $\times$ 10^−5/^oC. Coefficient of volume expansion of mercury is 1.82 $\times$ 10^-4/^oC.
    (A) 2.7 cm^3   <-- CORRECT
    (B) 2.5 cm^3
    (C) 2.3 cm^3
    (D) 2.9 cm^3
    Answer: A
    Explanation: coefficient of linear expansion of the glass ${\gamma _S} $ = 0.40 $\times$ 10^−5/^oC
Coefficient of volume expansion of mercury ${a_L} $ = 1.82 $\times$ 10^-4/^oC
The number of mercury overflows
$\Delta V = ({\gamma _L} - {\gamma _S})V\Delta \theta$
${\gamma _S} $ = 3α = 3 $\times$ 0.4 $\times$ 10^−5 = 0.12 $\times$ 10^−4
ΔV = (1.82−0.12) $\times$ 10^−4 $\times$ 200 $\times$ 80 = 2.7cm^3
mercury overflows = 2.7cm^3
